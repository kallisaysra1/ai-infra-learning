# Exercise 03: Enterprise AI Stakeholder Mapping

Produce a stakeholder map for the AI strategy from Exercise
02. The map is the operating tool for navigating approval +
adoption across the enterprise.

## What the deliverable contains

### 1. Stakeholder inventory

For each of these (and any others your org has), identify
the specific person / role:

- CEO.
- CFO.
- CIO / CTO.
- General Counsel + Privacy Officer.
- Heads of major business units (3–5 expected).
- Head of HR.
- Head of Communications.
- Internal Audit.
- Risk / Compliance committee.
- Board: chair, audit-committee chair, technology-committee
  chair if any.
- External: lead regulator, lead auditor.

### 2. Per-stakeholder analysis

For each, characterize:
- **What they care about** (their lens on the AI strategy).
- **What they fear** (their downside risk).
- **What they want from you** (the deliverable / commitment).
- **Their decision authority** on this strategy.
- **Likely position**: champion / supportive / neutral /
  skeptical / resistant.

### 3. RACI matrix

For 5–6 key strategy decisions (e.g., "centralize ML platform",
"adopt vendor X for foundation model serving", "ship regulatory
compliance program"), produce a RACI matrix.

### 4. Conversation order

Sequence: who do you talk to first, in what order, before the
board sees the strategy? Defend the ordering.

## Quality criteria

A passing map:
- Stakeholders are **named** (or by role) and characterized
  individually.
- The position assessments are honest (not all "supportive").
- The conversation order has a defensible logic.

A failing map:
- Generic ("the CIO will support technology investments").
- Treats everyone as equally important.
- No conversation sequencing.

## Reflection

- Which stakeholder will be most consequential and most
  ignored?
- Which stakeholder's position is your best estimate vs.
  hardest to predict?
- Who do you need to talk to *before* writing the strategy
  memo, not after?
