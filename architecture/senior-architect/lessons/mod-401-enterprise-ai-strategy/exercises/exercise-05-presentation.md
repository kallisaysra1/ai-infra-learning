# Exercise 05: Board Presentation on Enterprise AI Strategy

Produce the **board-facing presentation** for the strategy
developed in Exercises 02–04.

## Deliverable

- **10–12 slides**, or equivalent structured doc.
- **20 minutes spoken**, 40 minutes Q&A budget.

## Required slides

1. **Title + executive summary** (1 slide, 3 sentences).
2. **The opportunity** — why now.
3. **The strategy in one diagram**.
4. **The three bets** (centralization / talent / regulatory
   or your three).
5. **What we kill**.
6. **Resource ask** (dollars + headcount).
7. **Risks and mitigations** (top 3).
8. **Success criteria at 12 / 24 months**.
9. **Critical path + dependencies**.
10. **The first 90 days** (concrete actions).
11. **Asks of the board**.
12. **Backup slides** (financial detail, competitor analysis).

## Constraints

- Slides are mostly visual / minimal text.
- The talk track lives in your speaker notes.
- Every slide answers the question "so what?" — board members
  don't read; they ask "and?" until they get the actual point.

## Quality criteria

A passing presentation:
- A board member could understand the strategy from the
  slide titles alone.
- "What we kill" is real and uncomfortable.
- The resource ask is specific.
- Risks are honest.

A failing presentation:
- 60-slide everything-bagel deck.
- "Risks: we will manage them."
- "We will invest in AI." (Already obvious.)

## Reflection

- Which slide will the board push back on hardest?
- Which slide does the CFO read most carefully?
- If you had 5 minutes instead of 20, which 3 slides do you
  show?
