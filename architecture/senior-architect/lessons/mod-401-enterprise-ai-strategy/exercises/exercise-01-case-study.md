# Exercise 01: Enterprise AI Strategy Case Study

Find a published case study on an enterprise's AI strategy
(HBR, MIT Sloan, McKinsey, BCG, or a credible operator's
post-mortem on their AI program). Recent (post-2022)
preferred so the LLM era is in scope.

In a 1500–2000 word writeup:

- **Summarize the situation**: industry, scale, AI maturity
  starting point, strategic context.
- **Identify the strategic choices made**: build vs. buy,
  centralize vs. federate, vendor selection, organizational
  structure, sequencing.
- **Identify the constraints**: budget, talent, regulatory,
  legacy infrastructure, board pressure.
- **Critique trade-offs you'd have weighed differently**:
  what would you have done, and why? What would have gone
  worse?
- **Apply 3 lessons to your own organization**: be concrete
  — "we should reorganize" is not a lesson; "we should split
  the platform team from the applied-ML teams because of
  X" is.

## Quality criteria

A passing writeup:
- Cites a specific case study with author + publication.
- Identifies at least 5 distinct strategic choices.
- Critiques at least 2 of those choices substantively.
- Lessons are organization-specific (not generic).

A failing writeup:
- Summarizes without analysis.
- Treats every choice as obviously right (or wrong) in
  hindsight.
- Lessons are generic ("focus on data quality").

## Reflection

After writing, answer for yourself:
- Which strategic choice was hardest to evaluate?
- Which lesson, if your org adopts it next quarter, has the
  largest impact?
