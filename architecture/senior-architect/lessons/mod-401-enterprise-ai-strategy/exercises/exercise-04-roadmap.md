# Exercise 04: 24-Month Enterprise AI Strategy Roadmap

Translate the strategy memo (Exercise 02) into a 24-month
execution roadmap.

## What the deliverable contains

### 1. Phased plan (4 phases, 6 months each)

For each 6-month phase:
- **Goal**: 1 sentence.
- **Deliverables**: 5–7 specific things that ship.
- **Success criteria**: how you know the phase succeeded.
- **Dependencies**: what must be true before the phase
  starts.
- **Risks**: top 2.

### 2. Phase sequencing rationale

Why this phase first, that phase last?
- Reversibility (cheap-to-reverse decisions go first).
- Dependency order (foundations before higher layers).
- Regulatory deadlines.
- Organizational change capacity.

### 3. Resource allocation

Per phase: rough headcount (FTE) + dollar spend.

### 4. Critical-path identification

Identify the 3 critical-path items whose slip delays
everything downstream. For each, what's the early-warning
indicator that it's slipping?

### 5. Quarterly check-ins

Define the metrics that get reviewed quarterly:
- Operational metrics (what's shipped vs. plan).
- Adoption metrics (are ML teams using the platform).
- Business metrics (the strategy's downstream outcome).

## Quality criteria

A passing roadmap:
- Phases have **distinct goals**, not just "more of the
  previous."
- Sequencing logic survives questioning.
- The critical path is named and monitored.
- Resource estimates are credible.

A failing roadmap:
- "Phase 1: build the platform. Phase 2: scale the platform."
  (Same goal restated.)
- Fantasy timelines.
- No critical-path analysis.

## Reflection

- Which phase will the team most want to skip? Why?
- Which deliverable in Phase 1 has the largest downstream
  impact?
- The CFO asks "if we get half the budget, what gets cut?"
  Answer.
