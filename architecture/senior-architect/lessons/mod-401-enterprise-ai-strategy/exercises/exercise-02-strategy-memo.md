# Exercise 02: Enterprise AI Strategy Memo

Write a **board-ready strategy memo** (2 pages max) proposing
the enterprise's AI strategy for the next 24 months.

## Scenario

Your org is a 1,500-person company in a regulated industry
(financial services, healthcare, or critical infrastructure).
Current AI maturity is mixed: some pockets of strong ML
practice, much of the org is on opportunistic generative-AI
experimentation. The board has asked for a coherent strategy.

You have:
- ~$25M annual AI budget envelope.
- 40 ML / data engineers across the org.
- A new CIO who is open to consolidation but skeptical of
  bold bets.
- Three credible competitive threats from peers.
- One regulator's pending AI guidance with 12-month compliance
  pressure.

## Memo requirements

The memo addresses:

- **TL;DR** (3 sentences readable in 20 seconds).
- **Strategic direction**: what we'll commit to, what we
  won't, why.
- **The three bets** (centralization, talent, regulatory
  posture, or any three you choose).
- **What we kill**: explicitly name 1–2 things the org stops
  doing.
- **Resource allocation**: rough split of the $25M.
- **Success criteria**: what the world looks like in 24 months
  if this works.
- **Risks + mitigations**: top 3.

## Constraints

- Two pages. Hard limit.
- Finance language, not engineering jargon.
- Honest about uncertainty — overclaim and the board sees
  through it.

## Quality criteria

A passing memo:
- Has a defensible thesis a board member could argue for.
- Makes explicit trade-offs (kills something).
- Sequences the work realistically.
- Anticipates the obvious objections.

A failing memo:
- "We will invest in AI." (Everyone is.)
- Doesn't kill anything.
- Lists 10 priorities — meaning no priorities.
