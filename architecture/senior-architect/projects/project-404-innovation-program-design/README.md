# Project 404: Enterprise AI Innovation Program

## Executive Summary

**Project Type**: Innovation Operating Model Capstone
**Duration**: 50 hours
**Business Impact**: A repeatable system that converts AI
research into business value with measurable conversion rates.
**Scope**: Innovation portfolio, intake, pipeline, scaling, and
sunsetting across an entire enterprise.

### Project Overview

Design the **enterprise AI innovation program** — the operating
model, the intake process, the staged-gate pipeline, the metrics,
and the talent strategy that lets a Fortune 500 company turn
research into deployed products at a defensible conversion rate.

Most enterprises have an "AI Lab" that demos well and ships
poorly. The conversion rate from PoC to production is typically
single-digit percent. This project designs the operating model
that pushes that to 25%+ — without slowing down the experimentation
itself.

By completing this project, you will demonstrate:

- The ability to design a portfolio operating model.
- Familiarity with horizon-1/2/3 frameworks, Three Horizons,
  staged-gate processes, and ambidextrous organization theory.
- Pragmatic understanding of what kills enterprise innovation
  (handoffs, IT review queues, security gates without paths,
  talent silos).

---

## Scenario

**Helios Health Group** is a $35B integrated health system
(hospitals + payer + pharmacy) with a stated ambition to be
the "most AI-augmented health system in the US by 2030." The
CEO has authorized a $200M innovation program over five years.

The board's expectation: a defensible conversion rate, public
case studies, and at least two breakthrough products that
materially move revenue or cost.

Constraints:

- Clinical-AI work has FDA + state regulatory considerations.
- HIPAA + PHI handling sets a floor on data-access processes.
- Existing clinical staff are skeptical after past AI rollouts
  that failed in the wild.
- Competitive landscape: 4-5 other large health systems making
  similar moves.

---

## Project Objectives

1. **Portfolio framework** — Horizons 1/2/3, target allocation,
   sunsetting criteria.
2. **Intake + triage** — how ideas become projects, with weeks-not-
   months cycle time.
3. **Staged-gate pipeline** — discovery → PoC → pilot → scale →
   sustain, with quantitative graduation criteria.
4. **Talent + structure** — innovation team design, embedded vs.
   centralized, partnerships with academia + startups.
5. **Metrics + portfolio dashboard** — what success looks like
   per stage; what gets reported to the board.

## Project Deliverables

### 1. Innovation Operating Model Document (20-25 pages)

**Section 1: Strategic Frame** (3-4 pages)
- Why this program exists.
- Horizon allocation (e.g., 60% H1, 30% H2, 10% H3).
- Definition of "innovation" vs. "execution."

**Section 2: Portfolio Management** (4-5 pages)
- Portfolio composition rules.
- Investment phasing.
- Sunsetting criteria + how-we-kill-things process.
- Cross-portfolio dependencies.

**Section 3: Intake Process** (3-4 pages)
- Idea sources (employees, customers, partners, scanning).
- Triage criteria.
- Cycle time targets (idea → decision in <30 days).

**Section 4: Staged-Gate Pipeline** (5-6 pages)
- Five stages: Discovery, PoC, Pilot, Scale, Sustain.
- Each stage: entry criteria, exit criteria, max duration,
  funding range, decision authority.
- Gate review cadence + composition.

**Section 5: Organizational Design** (3-4 pages)
- Innovation team(s) location in the org chart.
- Embedded vs. central labs.
- Partnerships (academic, startup, vendor).
- Reporting lines + executive sponsorship.

**Section 6: Operating Cadence** (2-3 pages)
- Weekly, monthly, quarterly, annual rituals.
- Demo Days, review boards, all-hands.
- Cross-portfolio sync.

---

### 2. Staged-Gate Playbook (15-page document)

For each of five stages:

- Purpose.
- Entry criteria (gate questions).
- Exit criteria (quantitative graduation thresholds).
- Required deliverables (e.g., for PoC exit: signed pilot
  partner, defined success metric, technical risk assessment).
- Failure handling.
- Funding model.
- Anti-patterns to watch.

This is the document a program manager uses week to week.

---

### 3. Portfolio Dashboard Spec (5-page memo + wireframe)

What gets shown:

- Active projects by stage.
- Cycle-time distribution per stage.
- Stage conversion rates (90-day rolling).
- Top three risks across portfolio.
- $ committed vs. $ realized.
- Sunsetting log.

Wireframe of the dashboard (slide, Figma, or sketch is fine).

---

### 4. Talent Strategy (10-page document)

- Roles needed (innovation PM, applied scientist, ML engineer,
  clinical SME, regulatory liaison, product design, change agent).
- Hiring plan: who first, who later.
- Build vs. buy vs. partner per role.
- Academic partnerships (5 target institutions + rationale).
- Startup engagement model (corp-dev, scout, partner, acquire).
- Internal talent rotation program.

---

### 5. Three Case Studies (5 pages each, 15 pages total)

Three illustrative projects that demonstrate the operating model
end-to-end:

- **Case 1 — Sepsis early-warning model**. Horizon 1, clinical,
  high regulatory load. Walks through the full staged-gate journey
  including a near-miss at the pilot stage.
- **Case 2 — Insurance prior-authorization automation**. Horizon
  1, operational, high ROI but politically sensitive (jobs).
- **Case 3 — Pharmacy demand-sensing LLM agent**. Horizon 2,
  novel architecture, technical risk.

Each case includes: the intake decision, the staged-gate journey,
the gate-review outcomes, the metrics tracked, and the lessons.

---

### 6. Board Briefing Deck (15-20 slides)

What the board sees at the program launch and annually:

- The thesis.
- The portfolio shape.
- The operating model.
- The metrics.
- Year-1 milestones.
- Year-3 expected state.
- The risks.

---

### 7. 24-Month Launch Plan (5-page document)

Quarter-by-quarter rollout. Hiring milestones. First gate
reviews. First Demo Day. First sunsetting decision.

## Assessment Rubric

|---|---|---|
| Operating-model design | 25% | Operating model integrates with existing IT/risk/regulatory functions; not parallel-rail. |
