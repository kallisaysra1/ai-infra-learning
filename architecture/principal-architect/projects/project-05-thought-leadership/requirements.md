# Requirements — Thought Leadership Portfolio

This document is the authoritative requirements baseline for the portfolio. Every artifact you produce must trace to one or more requirement IDs here.

Convention:
- `TR-x` = thesis requirement
- `CR-x` = channel requirement (per channel)
- `OR-x` = operating-model requirement
- `ER-x` = ethics / employer requirement
- `MR-x` = measurement requirement
- `CON-x` = constraint
- `ASM-x` = assumption
- MoSCoW: **M** = Must, **S** = Should, **C** = Could, **W** = Won't (this cycle)

---

## 1. Scope

### 1.1 In scope

- Personal thesis statement (the public argument)
- Conference talk: pick one venue, submit CFP, prepare deck, deliver
- Technical paper / long-form essay: pick one venue, draft, revise, publish
- Open-source contribution: pick one project, contribution arc, ship first contribution
- Advisory engagement strategy: identify, structure, close 2 engagements
- 12-month content calendar with topic queue + buffer pieces
- Employer alignment plan (approval workflow, IP boundaries, legal review)
- Leverage metrics dashboard per channel
- Reflection memo + cycle-2 plan
- Audience-specific bio + speaker page + LinkedIn-equivalent profile

### 1.2 Out of scope (this cycle)

- Founding a company / leaving current employer (separate decision; this portfolio enables it but does not execute it)
- Book deal / book proposal (different timeline; aim for cycle 2–3 if at all)
- Podcast launch (high time cost, low compounding; defer unless thesis genuinely demands)
- Conference *organizing* (different skill set; defer)
- Personal-brand visual identity / professional photographer (do once, cheaply; not in critical path)
- Side business / paid courses (different ethics framing; defer to a separate strategy doc)

### 1.3 Explicitly **not** decided here

These positions are deferred to strategy drafting:

- Newsletter platform: Substack vs. ConvertKit vs. self-hosted
- Bio / speaker-page hosting: personal site, LinkedIn-equivalent, GitHub Pages
- Conference selection: tier-1 vs. tier-2 trade (you'll choose one)
- Paper venue: peer-reviewed (USENIX, ACM Queue) vs. company engineering blog vs. industry magazine
- Advisory deal structure preference: equity-heavy vs. cash-heavy vs. mixed

## 2. Thesis requirements

- **TR-1 (M)**: The thesis is a single sentence (≤ 25 words) plus a 2–3 paragraph defense.
- **TR-2 (M)**: The thesis is **defensible** — supported by ≥ 3 concrete examples from your work or recent industry; falsifiable in principle.
- **TR-3 (M)**: The thesis is **specific** — distinguishes you from peers; a peer can recite it accurately.
- **TR-4 (M)**: The thesis spans channels — the same thesis appears (with channel-appropriate framing) in the conference talk, the paper, the OSS contribution arc, the advisory pitches, and the content calendar.
- **TR-5 (S)**: The thesis has a 12-month evolution path — you expect it to refine, not pivot, as you publish.
- **TR-6 (S)**: The thesis has an **anti-thesis** — what would a thoughtful peer who disagrees say, and how do you handle it?

## 3. Channel requirements

### 3.1 Conference talk (M)

- **CR-1 (M)**: Pick one tier-1 venue (KubeCon, AI Engineer Summit, MLOps World, Data + AI Summit, QCon, Strange Loop, RAILS, USENIX SREcon, peer) with a defended choice.
- **CR-2 (M)**: Submit a CFP within the next CFP cycle (likely 6–9 months out from selection).
- **CR-3 (M)**: CFP submission includes: title, abstract (≤ 200 words), audience profile, takeaways (≥ 3 specific), speaker bio, prior speaking evidence (if any), slide outline (if requested).
- **CR-4 (M)**: Prepare 30–45 slide deck even if not yet accepted (the deck is the test of whether the talk is real).
- **CR-5 (S)**: Rehearse the talk live at least once before submission (internal talk, peer review session, meetup).
- **CR-6 (S)**: If rejected, capture the rejection feedback; resubmit a revised version to a different venue.

### 3.2 Technical paper / long-form essay (M)

- **CR-7 (M)**: Pick one venue with publication standards (USENIX, ACM Queue, IEEE Software, named company engineering blog with editorial review, named industry magazine, hosted personal essay with promotion plan).
- **CR-8 (M)**: Paper / essay length: ≥ 3,500 words (essay) or per venue's standards (paper).
- **CR-9 (M)**: The paper includes original evidence (data, case study, novel synthesis); not solely opinion.
- **CR-10 (M)**: The paper is **edited** — by a peer with editorial chops or by the venue's editor.
- **CR-11 (S)**: The paper is published within the cycle; if not (long review cycles), it is at least at editor's desk with timeline.

### 3.3 Open-source contribution (M)

- **CR-12 (M)**: Pick one project with maintainer recognition value (CNCF / LF AI / a project whose maintainers are publicly known in the AI infra community).
- **CR-13 (M)**: Read the project's CONTRIBUTING.md, RFC process, and recent merged PRs / issues to understand maintainer expectations.
- **CR-14 (M)**: Contribution arc: not a drive-by. Plan 3+ contributions over 6 months: a documentation fix or small PR (warm-up); a substantive PR addressing a real issue; a design proposal / RFC / SIG participation if the project supports it.
- **CR-15 (M)**: First contribution shipped (merged) within the cycle; second contribution in flight.
- **CR-16 (S)**: Maintainer recognition: named in a release note, given committer access, invited to SIG / WG, or otherwise publicly acknowledged.

### 3.4 Advisory engagements (M)

- **CR-17 (M)**: Identify 5–10 potential advisory targets (companies, founders); 2–4 active conversations; 2 engagements signed or in term-sheet stage.
- **CR-18 (M)**: Each engagement has: scope (what you do), time commitment (hours/quarter), compensation (cash and/or equity), conflict-of-interest review.
- **CR-19 (M)**: Each engagement is cleared by your employer's legal / ethics process.
- **CR-20 (S)**: At least one engagement is equity-bearing (early-stage); at least one is cash-bearing (more mature stage) — diversifies risk.
- **CR-21 (S)**: Advisory work is time-boxed (e.g., 4–8 hours/month per engagement); the portfolio is curated, not maximized.

### 3.5 Content calendar (M)

- **CR-22 (M)**: 12 months of content topics queued; minimum 1 piece every 4 weeks (12 pieces minimum); recommended every 2 weeks (24 pieces).
- **CR-23 (M)**: ≥ 4 buffer pieces drafted (for crunch weeks).
- **CR-24 (M)**: Each piece has a target channel (newsletter, blog, LinkedIn-equivalent, Twitter-equivalent long post, cross-post to multiple).
- **CR-25 (M)**: Topics ladder back to the thesis — a peer reviewing the calendar can identify the thesis from the topic list.
- **CR-26 (S)**: Each piece has a target audience (other senior engineers, CTOs, junior engineers, etc.) and a leverage metric.
- **CR-27 (S)**: Newsletter platform (or equivalent) chosen with subscriber-export portability assured.

## 4. Operating-model requirements

- **OR-1 (M)**: Time budget per week is documented and predictable: e.g., 4–6 hours/week for sustained content + bursts for talks / papers / OSS. Total ≤ 15% of work week on average.
- **OR-2 (M)**: A weekly cadence is established (e.g., Friday afternoon writing block); cadence survives normal work crunch.
- **OR-3 (M)**: A monthly review captures what was produced, what was deferred, leverage metrics, and reallocations.
- **OR-4 (M)**: A drafting workflow is in place (notes → outline → draft → revise → publish); each piece does not start from a blank page.
- **OR-5 (S)**: An editor / peer-review buddy is identified for paper / blog work.
- **OR-6 (S)**: A backup channel exists for paper / talk if a venue rejects (don't sit on completed work).

---

## 5. Ethics / employer requirements

- **ER-1 (M)**: All external publication is reviewed by your employer's process (legal, IP, PR as applicable) before publication.
- **ER-2 (M)**: No employer-proprietary information is published without explicit clearance — including code, internal numbers, internal design docs, customer names.
- **ER-3 (M)**: Advisory engagements are disclosed to your employer and cleared per their conflict-of-interest policy.
- **ER-4 (M)**: Advisory engagements do not compete with your employer; do not work on competing products without explicit consent.
- **ER-5 (M)**: Time on portfolio is honest — you do not under-report to your manager or over-report to a future employer.
- **ER-6 (M)**: Public statements honor non-disparagement, NDA, and similar obligations from current and prior employers.
- **ER-7 (S)**: A documented disclosure appears on your speaker / writer bio when relevant (e.g., "the author is an advisor to X").

## 6. Measurement requirements

- **MR-1 (M)**: Per-channel leverage metrics defined:
  - Conference talk: in-room attendance, video views, post-talk inbound (DMs, emails, follow-on opportunities)
  - Paper / essay: organic reads, citations, inbound, time on page
  - OSS contribution: PR merge rate, maintainer engagement (review thread depth), follow-on issues assigned
  - Advisory: engagement count, hours, revenue, founder NPS (informal)
  - Content calendar: subscriber growth, open rate, share rate, inbound generated
- **MR-2 (M)**: Metrics dashboard reviewed monthly; reallocation decisions made quarterly.
- **MR-3 (M)**: At cycle end (12 months), an explicit channel reallocation: which channel grows, which shrinks, which stops.
- **MR-4 (S)**: Sponsor (your CTO / manager) gets a quarterly summary in ≤ 1 page.

## 7. Constraints

- **CON-1 (M)**: Total time on portfolio ≤ 15% of work week on average (8 hours / 50-hour work week as a typical anchor). Bursts up to 25% for talk preparation are acceptable; sustained > 15% is not.
- **CON-2 (M)**: All publication and advisory work cleared by employer process.
- **CON-3 (M)**: Advisory income (if cash) reported per employer policy; equity disclosed per IRS / SEC obligations as applicable.
- **CON-4 (M)**: No use of employer IP, code, customer data, or internal-only material in external publication without explicit clearance.
- **CON-5 (M)**: Conference travel: paid by venue / employer / personally, per employer policy; not a reason to take or decline a venue.
- **CON-6 (S)**: Tooling preference: keep tools sustainable and low-friction. Substack > custom CMS for a newsletter if you're not already maintaining a blog. Use what you'll keep using in cycle 2.
- **CON-7 (S)**: Brand: your name is the brand. Avoid pseudonyms unless you have a specific reason; pseudonyms compound less reputation per piece.

## 8. Assumptions

- **ASM-1**: Your employer continues to sponsor (≥ 15% time approval) through cycle 1.
- **ASM-2**: You have at least one piece of recent technical work that could serve as the spine of a paper / talk (you are not starting from zero).
- **ASM-3**: Your employer's legal team is responsive (≤ 2 weeks for clearance on standard items).
- **ASM-4**: Tier-1 conferences accept ~10–20% of submissions in your topic area; you may need to submit to 2–3 to get accepted at one.
- **ASM-5**: Your network includes ≥ 2 people who would peer-review a paper draft and ≥ 5 founders you could approach for advisory conversations.
- **ASM-6**: You are willing to be publicly visible — this includes accepting public critique, social attention, and the optics of changing jobs / earning advisory income.

If ASM-6 wobbles, the entire project's scope changes. Discuss with sponsor.

## 9. Acceptance criteria by major deliverable

### D1. Thought leadership strategy document
- 15–25 pages
- Covers thesis + 5 channels + operating model + measurement + ethics
- Maps each artifact to ≥ 1 requirement ID
- Reviewed by ≥ 1 peer + sponsor

### D2. Thesis statement
- Single sentence (≤ 25 words) + 2–3 paragraph defense
- ≥ 3 concrete supporting examples
- Anti-thesis named with response

### D3. Conference talk proposal (CFP + deck)
- CFP submitted to a named tier-1 venue
- 30–45 slide deck drafted
- Rehearsed at least once
- Acceptance OR substantive rejection feedback to revise

### D4. Technical paper / essay
- ≥ 3,500 words (essay) or per venue standards (paper)
- Original evidence included
- Edited by ≥ 1 peer or venue editor
- Published OR at editor's desk with timeline

### D5. Open-source contribution plan + first contribution
- Project selected with rationale
- Contribution arc planned (3+ contributions over 6 months)
- First contribution merged
- Second contribution in flight or queued

### D6. Advisory engagement strategy + 2 signed
- 5–10 targets identified
- 2 engagements signed (or 1 signed + 1 term sheet)
- Legal cleared
- Time commitment ≤ 8 hours/month/engagement

### D7. 12-month content calendar + buffer
- 12 months of topics (≥ 12 pieces minimum)
- ≥ 4 buffer pieces drafted
- Each piece: target channel, target audience, ties to thesis
- Newsletter platform chosen and set up

### D8. Employer alignment plan
- Approval workflow documented
- IP boundaries documented
- Sponsor signoff captured

### D9. Leverage metrics dashboard
- Per-channel metrics defined with collection mechanism
- Monthly review cadence established
- Q1 baseline captured

### D10. Reflection memo
- Written at end of cycle (4–8 pages)
- What worked, what didn't
- Channel reallocation for cycle 2
- 12-month outcomes vs. goals

## 10. Non-requirements (and why)

These are intentionally **not** requirements:

- **A book deal**: out (long lead time; defer to cycle 2 if at all)
- **A podcast**: out (high time cost, low compounding for senior engineers; defer)
- **A personal brand campaign**: out (the work is the brand; campaigns are for products)
- **Speaking at non-aligned venues**: out (saying yes to every speaking ask kills the time budget; be selective)
- **Twitter / X follower count goals**: out (vanity metric; quality of inbound is the actual measure)
- **Becoming a "creator"**: out (you are an engineer who publishes, not a content business)
- **Quitting your job**: out (this project may enable that; it does not require it)

Document this list visibly. The discipline of saying no to plausible-but-distracting work is half the project.
