## Answer Key

### Section 1: Leadership Principles
1. C - Tech leads multiply impact by enabling others
2. B - Hear both perspectives and underlying concerns
3. C - Blameless post-mortem and systemic improvements
4. Short Answer - Should include: putting team needs first, removing blockers, enabling others' success. Examples: reviewing code to unblock others, taking on difficult tasks no one wants, advocating for team resources
5. B - Bottleneck leader

### Section 2: Mentorship & Coaching
6. C - Ask about requirements and help evaluate trade-offs
   Explanation should mention: mentoring is about teaching how to think, not what to think
7. B - 70% on-the-job, 20% mentoring/social, 10% formal training
8. B - Socratic method
9. Short Answer - Should include differences like:
   - Mentoring = skill development; Sponsorship = career advocacy
   - Mentor gives advice; Sponsor opens doors and vouches for you
   - Mentoring is about growth; Sponsorship is about opportunity
10. C - Ask guiding questions and only provide hints

### Section 3: Code Review
11. B - Request changes with detailed explanation
    Rationale: Performance issues should be addressed; feedback should be specific and constructive regardless of seniority
12. C - Knowledge sharing and quality improvement
13. D - Drive-by reviewing
14. B - False (quality standards should be maintained; feedback can be constructive)

### Section 4: Decision Making
15. A - DACI framework
    Reasoning: Clear ownership needed with tight deadline; DACI ensures decision gets made while incorporating input
16. B - Approver makes the final decision
17. Short Answer - Should explain: voicing disagreement clearly, then fully committing once decision is made. Appropriate when: decision is made after fair process, stakes are high, team needs to move forward unified
18. B - Confirmation bias
19. A - One-way can't be reversed; two-way can be easily reversed

### Section 5: Communication & Consensus
20. B - Business case with ROI and strategic alignment
    Key elements: cost-benefit analysis, risk mitigation, competitive positioning, measurable outcomes
21. D - Both focus on business impact AND keep it brief
22. A - Assuming others have the same knowledge you do
23. Short Answer - Should explain: asking "why" repeatedly to get to root causes. Example: use it to uncover underlying concerns when team resists a technology choice, revealing real issues (e.g., fear of learning curve, past bad experiences)
24. C - Create space for unheard perspectives
    Why: Inclusive, non-confrontational way to invite participation; respects their autonomy
25. C - Ensuring everyone feels heard and commits to the decision
