## Exercise 5: Write an MLOps Team Charter (60 minutes)

**Objective**: Articulate what an MLOps function owns + how it serves the broader org.

### Background

Engineering excellence doesn't matter if nobody knows what your team does or
when to engage you. The charter is the first artifact every successful MLOps
team writes.

### Tasks

1. Draft `CHARTER.md` covering:
   - **Mission** (1 sentence)
   - **What we own** (3-5 services + outcomes)
   - **What we DO NOT own** (deliberate scope limits)
   - **Engagement model**: self-serve / office hours / project intake
   - **Support tiers** (page-worthy vs Slack vs queue)
   - **Success metrics** (model deploy lead time, MTBF, adoption %)
   - **Failure modes to avoid** (gatekeeper trap, custom-everywhere, etc.)

2. Run it past one peer for feedback.

3. Iterate; settle on a final version.

### Deliverable

`CHARTER.md` (~1500-2000 words).

### Acceptance criteria

- Clear scope boundaries (the "do not own" list is as important as "do own")
- Metrics are quantitative + tracked
- Engagement model is concrete (not "engage us early!")
