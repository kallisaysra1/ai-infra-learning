# Capstone Self-Assessment

> The capstone is **not** quiz-based. Instead, self-assessment
> happens through the readiness checklist (before starting) and
> the grading rubric (after completing the portfolio).

## Before starting

Use [`readiness-checklist.md`](./readiness-checklist.md) to
verify you have the prerequisite skills from Modules 01-11.

## After completing the portfolio

Use [`grading-rubric.md`](./grading-rubric.md) to score your
own work, or have a peer / instructor score it.

## Quick self-test

If you want a brief sanity check before starting:

### Q1
Can you, without notes, draw the relationship between a
threat model, an architecture, a control catalog, a
detection ruleset, and an IR procedure? If you can't
articulate why each depends on the previous, return to the
relevant module before attempting the capstone.

### Q2
Can you, in 60 seconds, explain to a non-technical
executive why an AI infrastructure security engineer is a
distinct role from a generic infrastructure security
engineer? The capstone's communication exercise requires
this kind of compression.

### Q3
Can you name three threats that the OWASP ML Top 10 covers
and three threats it doesn't? The capstone's threat model
requires going beyond the obvious catalog items.

If you're confident on all three, proceed to
[Exercise 01](./exercises/exercise-01-threat-model-and-risk-register.md).
If not, the relevant modules are: 01 (Q3), 11 (Q1), 07 (Q2).

## Capstone-specific reflection (after completion)

After producing the full portfolio, answer for yourself:

1. **Which artifact am I most proud of?** Why?
2. **Which artifact would I rewrite if I had another week?**
3. **Which decision would I defend most strongly under
   questioning?** Why?
4. **Which gap in my own knowledge did this surface most
   clearly?**
5. **What's the next thing I want to learn?**

These questions don't have right answers. They're the basis
for the post-capstone interview prep.
