## Capstone-specific references

A small set of resources useful specifically for the synthesis
work:

### Stakeholder communication

- **Storytelling with Data** by Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic — for
  the CFO brief.
- **Style Guide for Federal Plain Language Guidelines**
  [plainlanguage.gov](https://www.plainlanguage.gov/) — for
  the regulator-facing documentation.

### Customer-facing security documentation

- **Cloud Security Alliance CAIQ** (Consensus Assessments
  Initiative Questionnaire) — what hospital customer CISOs
  often ask.
  [cloudsecurityalliance.org/research/cloud-controls-matrix/](https://cloudsecurityalliance.org/research/cloud-controls-matrix/)

- **Vendor Security Alliance** (VSA) questionnaire
  [vendorsecurityalliance.org](https://www.vendorsecurityalliance.org/)

### EU AI Act technical documentation

- **EU AI Act Annex IV** — the technical documentation
  requirements.
  [eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)

- **EU AI Act Compass** (Future of Life Institute)
  [eu-ai-act-compass.futureoflife.org](https://eu-ai-act-compass.futureoflife.org/)
  Practical guidance on the obligations per actor type.

## What's deliberately not on this list

- **New material.** The capstone doesn't introduce new
  concepts. Anything new belongs in a future module, not in
  the synthesis.
- **Vendor security-platform marketing.** The capstone is
  about building defensible designs, not picking products.
- **Pre-built capstone "solutions."** The capstone is
  individual work; a model answer would defeat its purpose.
