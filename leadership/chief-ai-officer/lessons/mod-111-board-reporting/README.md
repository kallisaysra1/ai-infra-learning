# Module 111 — Board Reporting and Risk Appetite

> Module 111 of the Chief AI Officer track. Where
> everything below rolls up to executive
> accountability — the quarterly report, the risk
> appetite statement, the materiality framework, the
> CAO at the board table. This is the module that
> determines whether the program is *visible* at the
> top of the organisation in ways that matter.

## What you will leave with

After working through this module you should be able to:

1. Distinguish what boards need from CAO reporting
   from what they don't — and avoid the common
   over-reporting failure mode.
2. Draft an AI risk appetite statement that survives
   board review and operates against actual
   decisions.
3. Author a quarterly AI report that the Board Risk
   Committee can act on — short, structured,
   honest.
4. Apply a materiality framework that determines
   when an AI matter reaches the board vs. stays at
   the operational level.
5. Operate the CAO at the board table — what to
   say, what to ask for, how to handle pressure.
6. Conduct an annual program self-assessment that
   surfaces meaningful findings rather than
   confirming the program is operating.
