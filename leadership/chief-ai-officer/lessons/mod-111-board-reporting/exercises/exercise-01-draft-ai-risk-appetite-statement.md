# Exercise 01 — Draft an AI Risk Appetite Statement

**Estimated time**: 3 hours
**Deliverable**: AI risk appetite statement (≤ 3 pages)

---

## The scenario

You are the CAO at **Halverston Capital**. The
Board Risk Committee has asked you to draft an
AI risk appetite statement for adoption at the
next quarterly meeting. The statement will be
the foundation document for Halverston's AI
program — every other governance artifact
derives from it.

## Your assignment

Produce a draft AI risk appetite statement with
the structure from §2.3:

### Section 1 — Preamble (≤ ¼ page)

- What AI risk means at Halverston.
- What this statement does (and does not do).
- The relationship to Halverston's enterprise
  risk appetite.

### Section 2 — Risk categories (≤ ¼ page)

Drawn from Halverston's AI risk taxonomy (from
mod-103 Ex-01 reference). Brief listing.

### Section 3 — For each category, the appetite (≤ 1¾ pages)

For each of Halverston's 8 top-level categories
(model performance, bias and fairness, transparency
and explainability, privacy and data, security,
vendor and third-party AI, market and conduct,
strategic and reputational), express the
appetite using §2.4 patterns — risk-class
framing, boundary language, and threshold
language where appropriate.

Each category gets one paragraph + one boundary
or threshold expression.

### Section 4 — Escalation process (≤ ¼ page)

What happens when a case approaches or exceeds
the boundaries.

### Section 5 — Review cadence (≤ ¼ page)

When the board reconsiders the statement.

## Constraints

- The statement must be **board-readable**. A
  board member without specialised AI knowledge
  should be able to read and act on it.
- Each category's appetite expression must
  combine **at least two** of the §2.4 patterns
  (risk-class + boundary, or risk-class +
  threshold, etc.).
- At least three categories must include
  **specific quantitative thresholds**
  (percentage points, dollar amounts, count
  thresholds).
- The bias-and-fairness category must
  acknowledge the mod-105 impossibility result
  per §2.5 — naming which fairness conception
  takes priority at Halverston.
- The statement must specify the escalation
  process from §4 (mod-110) integration —
  cases approaching boundaries enter the
  incident-classification taxonomy.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Structure — five sections per §2.3 | 15% |

| Quantitative thresholds where appropriate | 15% |
