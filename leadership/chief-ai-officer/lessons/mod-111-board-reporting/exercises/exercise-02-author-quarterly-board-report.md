# Exercise 02 — Author a Quarterly Board Report

**Estimated time**: 3 hours
**Deliverable**: Quarterly board report (≤ 5 pages)

---

## The scenario

You are the CAO at **Northfield Mutual**. The Q2
Board Risk Committee meeting is in two weeks. You
need to author the quarterly AI report. The
quarter included:

- The Site H-12 bias incident (mod-110 Ex-01).
- The state insurance regulator inquiry resulting
  from the incident.
- The EU AI Act NCA notification.
- Three program-improvement milestones (the new
  training-data refresh review gate; the
  improved monitoring sensitivity; the
  formalised patient-notification authority).
- The acceptance of one residual risk.
- An ask for board ratification on an updated
  bias-monitoring threshold.

## Your assignment

Produce a quarterly board report following the
structure from §3.1. Use the H-12 incident
context throughout to ground the report.

### Section 1 — Risk posture summary (≤ ½ page)

Aggregate view of the program by risk category,
with change-from-last-quarter indicators.

### Section 2 — Material changes (≤ ½ page)

What has changed in the trailing quarter that the
board should know.

### Section 3 — Material exceptions (≤ 1 page)

Cases outside risk appetite, with response and
timeline. The H-12 incident's bias-monitoring
threshold crossing is one. The regulator
notification activity is another. Be specific.

### Section 4 — Material decisions pending (≤ ½ page)

What the board needs to decide.

### Section 5 — Material changes ahead (≤ ½ page)

What's foreseeable.

### Section 6 — Asks (≤ ½ page)

Specific asks. Per §3.2, at least one ask is
required. The bias-monitoring threshold update
is the natural ask; identify others.

### Appendices (any length, treated as reference)

Include reference material the board does not
need to read but may consult — incident register,
metrics, vendor register.

## Constraints

- The main report is **3-5 pages**. Appendices
  do not count toward this limit but the report
  cover is the 3-5 pages.
- Per §3.3, the report must be **honest** about
  the H-12 incident. Not softened.
- Per §3.2, at least one specific ask is
  required.
- The report must avoid §1.3 anti-patterns —
  no operational metrics in detail, no
  technology details, no educational content.
- The risk posture summary must use the
  appetite categories from Exercise 01's
  statement.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| All six sections present | 15% |
| Risk posture — uses appetite categories | 15% |
| H-12 incident honestly addressed | 25% |
| Material asks — specific, at least one | 15% |
| Avoidance of §1.3 anti-patterns | 15% |
| Length discipline — 3-5 pages | 15% |
