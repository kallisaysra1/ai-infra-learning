# Exercise 05 — Annual Program Self-Assessment

**Estimated time**: 3 hours
**Deliverable**: Self-assessment + recommendations (≤ 4 pages)

---

## The scenario

You are the CAO at **Halverston Capital**. End of
your first full year as CAO. The annual self-
assessment is due. The Audit Committee will
read it; the AI Risk Council will use it as
input to next year's planning.

The trailing year included:

- Operationalizing the AI Risk Council and AI
  Review Board.
- Implementing the trust architecture per the
  mod-106 Ex-05 partner pattern.
- The first cross-LOB tabletop exercise (per
  mod-110 Ex-03).
- One material incident (a near-miss avoided
  via the early-detection cadence).
- The ISO 42001 certification process (in
  progress).
- A material vendor change in the LLM
  underlying wealth advisory.
- Hiring 6 net new roles in the CAO function.

## Your assignment

Produce a self-assessment with the structure
from §6.2:

### Section 1 — Program maturity (≤ ¾ page)

Rating against NIST AI RMF maturity model
across the four functions (GOVERN, MAP,
MEASURE, MANAGE). For each:

- Current maturity level.
- Specific evidence.
- Gap to next maturity level.

### Section 2 — Operating effectiveness (≤ ¾ page)

- Are the controls operating?
- What does the evidence show?
- Specific incidents (the near-miss);
  specific exercises (the tabletop).
- Honest finding on what worked, what didn't.

### Section 3 — Coverage gaps (≤ ½ page)

What's not yet covered that should be. Top 3
gaps with specific recommendations.

### Section 4 — Investment posture (≤ ½ page)

- Where the program invested in year 1.
- Where the program underinvested.
- Where year 2 investment should focus.

### Section 5 — Leadership and talent (≤ ½ page)

- Year 1 hiring outcomes.
- Capability gaps remaining.
- Talent risks (key person dependencies,
  market competition for AI roles).

### Section 6 — Cultural integration (≤ ½ page)

- Is the program integrated or operating in
  parallel?
- Evidence either way.
- Recommendations to deepen integration.

### Section 7 — Specific recommendations (≤ ¼ page)

A prioritised list of what year 2 will do
differently.

## Constraints

- The assessment must be **honest**. Per
  §6.3, the honesty test: would the CAO be
  comfortable showing this to a peer CAO at a
  similar institution?
- Each section must include **specific
  evidence**, not assertions.
- At least one section must surface a
  **substantive** finding — something
  meaningful, not a trivial improvement.
- The recommendations must be **specific** —
  same discipline as mod-110 PIR
  recommendations.
- The CAO function's underperformance, if
  any, must be honestly named. Self-
  assessments that name only successes are
  not credible.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| All six dimensions covered | 20% |

| Operating effectiveness — honest | 15% |
| Coverage gaps — specific | 15% |
| Investment posture — substantive | 10% |
| Cultural integration — honest | 10% |
| Recommendations — specific, prioritised | 10% |
| Honesty test would pass | 5% |
