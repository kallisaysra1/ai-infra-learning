# Exercise 03 — Build a Control-Coverage Gap Analysis

**Estimated time**: 3 hours
**Deliverable**: Gap analysis + remediation roadmap (≤ 3 pages)

---

## The scenario

You are the CAO at **Halverston Capital**. Per
mod-108 Ex-04, Halverston is approaching ISO 42001
certification. The certification auditor will
perform a gap analysis against Annex A. The CRO
has asked you to perform the **internal gap
analysis first** so that Halverston can address
the gaps before the auditor arrives.

## Your assignment

### Section 1 — Approach (≤ ¼ page)

State:

- The scope of the gap analysis (Annex A's 10
  control objectives, all controls within).
- The method (mapping existing program controls to
  Annex A controls; identifying gaps where no
  mapping exists).
- The maturity scale you use to rate each control
  (e.g., Not Present / Partial / In Place /
  Mature).

### Section 2 — The mapping (≤ 1½ pages)

For each of Annex A's 10 control objectives,
provide a summary table:

| Annex A control objective | Halverston coverage | Maturity rating | Gap notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| A.2 Policies related to AI | ... | ... | ... |
| A.3 Internal organisation | ... | ... | ... |
| ... | | | |

For each objective, identify:

- Which Halverston controls map to this objective.
- The maturity rating overall for this objective.
- Specific gaps (Annex A sub-controls without a
  Halverston control).

### Section 3 — The top 5 gaps (≤ ¾ page)

For the five most material gaps identified:

- **Gap description.**
- **Annex A reference**.
- **Halverston-specific implication** (why this
  matters at Halverston given its three LOBs).
- **Remediation activity** (the specific work to
  close the gap).
- **Owner** (named role at Halverston).
- **Target close date.**

### Section 4 — Remediation roadmap (≤ ½ page)

A timeline showing:

- Pre-audit (next 6 months): which gaps will be
  closed.
- Post-certification (months 6–12 after audit):
  which gaps are deferred and why.
- The acceptance posture for un-closed gaps:
  what the CAO accepts as residual.

## Constraints

- The gap analysis must address **all 10 control
  objectives**.
- The top 5 gaps must be substantive — not
  trivial ("we need to update one document").
- Each gap must have a **specific owner** and
  **target close date**.
- The acceptance posture for un-closed gaps must
  be defensible to the audit committee.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| All 10 control objectives covered | 25% |
| Top 5 gaps — substantive, Halverston-specific | 25% |
| Owners and target dates specific | 20% |
| Remediation roadmap — realistic | 15% |
| Acceptance posture — defensible | 10% |
| Length discipline — ≤ 3 pages | 5% |
