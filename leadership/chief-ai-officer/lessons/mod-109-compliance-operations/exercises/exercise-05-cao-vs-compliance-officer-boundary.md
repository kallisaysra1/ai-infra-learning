# Exercise 05 — Resolve a CAO × Compliance Officer Boundary Dispute

**Estimated time**: 3 hours
**Deliverable**: Resolution memo + boundary diagram (≤ 3 pages)

---

## The scenario

You are the CAO at **Sentinel Mutual Bank**.
Sentinel's Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) has
proposed that the entire AI program's regulatory
reporting be folded into Sentinel's existing
compliance reporting process — same intake, same
forms, same approvers, same outbound channels. The
CCO's argument: regulators want consistent
responses from Sentinel; spreading regulatory
engagement across multiple functions (CCO,
CAO function, CISO, MRM) creates risk of
inconsistency. The CCO has been at Sentinel for
twelve years and has the CEO's confidence on
regulatory matters.

You (the CAO) have specific concerns. Regulatory
engagement on AI matters is *not* equivalent to
classical compliance engagement. The EU AI Act,
NYDFS Part 500 AI amendments, and CFPB AI-related
inquiries require AI-specific expertise the CCO's
organisation does not currently have. Folding AI
regulatory engagement into the CCO's process
risks degrading the responses — and potentially
producing the very inconsistency the CCO is
trying to avoid (Sentinel says one thing about
AI to one regulator and something else to
another).

You also have a structural concern: the CAO's
contributions to AI compliance (control mapping,
continuous evidence, AI risk taxonomy) are
embedded in the AI Risk Council and the AI Review
Board governance machinery. Subordinating them to
the CCO's reporting process risks losing the
operational discipline.

The CEO has asked you to **respond to the CCO's
proposal** in a memo, addressing the CCO's
concerns substantively while preserving the
AI-program-specific machinery where it matters.

## Your assignment

Produce two artifacts.

### Artifact 1 — Resolution memo (2 pages)

A memo that:

1. **States the resolution** in the first
   sentence.
2. **Steel-mans the CCO's position** — fairly, in
   the CCO's voice, addressing the consistency
   concern substantively.
3. **Steel-mans your own position** — addressing
   the AI-specificity concern and the operational
   discipline concern.
4. **Explains the resolution** — including which
   elements of each position survive and which
   yield.
5. **Names the operational mechanics**: who owns
   what part of regulatory engagement, how
   coordination happens.
6. **Acknowledges the loss** — what each side
   gives up.
7. **Names the precedent** — future similar
   boundary questions will be guided.

### Artifact 2 — Boundary diagram (1 page)

Per §6 framework, a diagram showing:

- The CCO's scope on AI-related compliance.
- The CAO function's scope on AI-related
  compliance.
- The intersection and how it operates.
- Specific scenarios (regulator letter,
  examination, settlement) and how they route.

## Constraints

- The resolution must be **defensible to both
  the CCO and the CEO**.
- The CCO's consistency concern must be
  addressed substantively — not dismissed.
- Your AI-specificity concern must be
  substantiated with specific examples.
- The mechanics must not create **parallel
  regulator-facing functions**. The CCO's
  concern about this would be valid; the
  resolution must avoid it.
- The boundary diagram must address at least
  **three specific scenarios**.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Resolution stated up front | 10% |
| CCO steel-man — fair, substantive | 20% |
| CAO steel-man — substantive | 15% |
| Resolution reasoning — what survives + yields | 25% |
| Mechanics — no parallel functions | 15% |
| Boundary diagram — three+ scenarios | 10% |
| Acknowledged loss | 5% |

## Where to submit

`ai-infra-chief-ai-officer-solutions/modules/mod-109-compliance-operations/exercise-05-cao-vs-compliance-officer-boundary/SOLUTION.md`

Reference solution adopts a **single-intake**
resolution: all regulatory inbound and outbound on
AI matters flows through the CCO's intake process
for consistency, but AI-substantive content is
authored by the CAO function. The CCO chairs joint
response reviews; the CAO function maintains the
AI-specific machinery (controls, evidence, the
AI risk taxonomy) operational below the regulator-
facing layer. Precedent: AI-specific regulatory
content follows this split-authorship pattern.
