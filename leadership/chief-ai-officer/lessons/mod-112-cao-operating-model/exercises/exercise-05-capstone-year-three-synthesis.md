# Exercise 05 — Capstone: Year-3 Program Synthesis

**Estimated time**: 5 hours
**Deliverable**: Comprehensive year-3 program view (≤ 6 pages)

---

## The capstone

This is the closing exercise of the foundational
track. You are the CAO of any of the fictional
institutions used throughout the curriculum
(Halverston, Tessera, Northfield, Aldwych,
Sentinel, Cardinal, Kerridge, Crestbridge, or
Trillium). End of year 3. The Board has asked
for a comprehensive view of where the program
stands.

This exercise is more open-ended than the
others. It is the synthesis of what you have
learned across the track.

## Your assignment

Produce a year-3 program synthesis. The
synthesis is not a board report (mod-111 Ex-02
is); it is a more comprehensive document — the
Board's CAO-of-the-year self-summary. Sections:

### Section 1 — Where the program is (≤ 1 page)

The current state across the dimensions you
choose to highlight. Drawn from across the
track: maturity (mod-111 §6); operating
effectiveness (mod-109 + mod-110); risk
posture (mod-111); incident history
(mod-110); compliance state (mod-109); talent
state (mod-112 §6).

Honest assessment — neither overclaiming nor
undue modesty.

### Section 2 — What changed since year 2 (≤ ¾ page)

The substantive changes in year 3. New
investments; new capabilities; new gaps; new
external context.

### Section 3 — What works that is durable (≤ ¾ page)

The structures, practices, and patterns from
the past three years that have proven durable
and should be preserved through changes ahead.

### Section 4 — What's fragile (≤ ¾ page)

Honest naming of what is fragile in the
program. Key-person risks; concentrated vendor
dependencies; controls that operate but haven't
been substantively tested; capacity bottlenecks.

### Section 5 — What we got wrong (≤ ¾ page)

The choices the CAO and the program have made
over three years that the CAO would, in
hindsight, have made differently. This is the
hardest section to author honestly; it is also
the section that distinguishes the year-3
synthesis from a year-3 marketing piece.

### Section 6 — Year-4 and beyond (≤ ¾ page)

Forward look. Not a year-4 plan; a posture and
direction. What is the program aiming to do?
What capabilities does it need to develop?
What is the CAO's intentional posture for the
next phase?

### Section 7 — Personal reflection (≤ ¼ page, optional)

If you choose: the CAO's reflection on the
three years. What has the role required of you
personally? What has the role given you? Where
do you go from here?

### Section 8 — What the board should know (≤ ¼ page)

Three to five things the Board should know
that aren't otherwise highlighted in the
report.

## Constraints

- The document must be **honest** per the §6.3
  peer-CAO test from mod-111.
- "What we got wrong" must contain **at least 3
  substantive items**. Programs that surface no
  errors in three years are not credible.
- "What's fragile" must be substantive — not
  trivial gaps. Real fragilities.
- "What's durable" must be specific — what
  about the structures has proven robust.
- The synthesis must not be a marketing piece.
  It must read as if the CAO is writing to a
  successor who will inherit the program.
- The personal reflection section is **optional**
  — programs that include it as a checkbox
  rather than as substance should leave it out.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Where the program is — honest, multi-dimensional | 15% |
| What changed — substantive | 10% |
| What's durable — specific | 15% |
| What's fragile — honest, substantive | 20% |
| What we got wrong — at least 3 substantive items | 25% |
| Year-4 and beyond — posture and direction, not plan | 10% |
| Reads as written-to-successor, not marketing | 5% |
