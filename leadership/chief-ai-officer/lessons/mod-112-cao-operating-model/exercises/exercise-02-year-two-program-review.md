# Exercise 02 — Author a Year-2 Program Review

**Estimated time**: 3 hours
**Deliverable**: Year-2 program review with priorities (≤ 3 pages)

---

## The scenario

You are the CAO at **Tessera Bank**. End of year 2.
The year-1 self-assessment (per mod-111 Ex-05
pattern) rated the program at mid-Developing
across most NIST functions. Year 2 has now
completed; you need to author the year-2
program review and set year-3 priorities.

## Your assignment

Produce a year-2 program review with:

### Section 1 — Year-2 results against the year-2 plan (≤ ¾ page)

Per §3.1, the six things year 2 should
accomplish. For each:

- The year-2 target.
- The actual result.
- The gap (if any).

### Section 2 — Maturity progression (≤ ½ page)

NIST maturity per function — where the program
was at start of year 2 and where it is now.

### Section 3 — Year-2 trap addressed (≤ ½ page)

Per §3.3, the year-2 trap is trying to fix every
year-1 error comprehensively. Address whether
year 2 fell into this trap and how it was
navigated.

### Section 4 — First material incident response (≤ ½ page)

Per §3.4, most programs face their first
material incident in year 2. Address either:

- The incident that occurred, the response, and
  the post-incident review findings; OR
- The near-miss that didn't materialize as
  material — and what the program would have
  done.

### Section 5 — Year-3 priorities (≤ ½ page)

Per §4.1, the five things year 3 should
accomplish — calibrated to Tessera's
year-2 maturity state. The priorities for
year 3 in priority order.

### Section 6 — Stalling-watch (≤ ¼ page)

Per §4.3, the warning signs of year-3 stalling.
For each, an honest assessment of whether
Tessera is showing the symptom.

## Constraints

- The year-2 results must be **honest** — per
  §3 of mod-111. Programs that show only
  successes are not credible.
- The maturity progression must be **specific**
  — not "improved" but "Level 2 (Developing) to
  Level 3 (Defined) on GOVERN".
- The first-material-incident treatment must be
  substantive — name the incident, the
  response, the findings.
- Year-3 priorities must be **prioritised** — at
  most 5, with reasoning for the priority order.
- The stalling-watch must include **at least one
  honest finding** that a symptom is present —
  programs that show no warning signs are
  almost certainly not seeing themselves
  clearly.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Year-2 results — honest treatment of all six | 25% |
| Maturity progression — specific | 15% |
| Year-2 trap addressed substantively | 10% |
| First material incident treatment | 15% |
| Year-3 priorities — prioritised | 20% |
| Stalling-watch — at least one honest finding | 15% |
