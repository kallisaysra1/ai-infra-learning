# Exercise 05 — Organizational Incident-Readiness Assessment

**Estimated time**: 3 hours
**Deliverable**: Readiness assessment (≤ 3 pages)

---

## The scenario

You are advising **Aldwych Health** (continuation
from mod-101 / mod-102 / mod-103 / mod-104 — the
UK healthcare system). Aldwych has implemented the
governance, monitoring, classification, and
notification machinery from earlier modules. The
Audit Committee has asked for an **honest
readiness assessment** before the system is
considered operational.

## Your assignment

Produce a readiness assessment with:

### Section 1 — Scope and method (≤ ¼ page)

- What is being assessed (Aldwych's AI incident
  response readiness across people, process,
  technology).
- The method used (interview, document review,
  tabletop, evidence sampling).
- The readiness scale used.

### Section 2 — The dimensions assessed (≤ 2 pages)

For at least **eight** dimensions of readiness:

1. **Detection capability** — can incidents be
   detected reliably and quickly.
2. **Classification capability** — can the team
   classify per the taxonomy without
   negotiation.
3. **Containment capability** — can the team
   contain proportionate to the incident.
4. **Investigation capability** — can the team
   investigate with the §5.1 discipline.
5. **Notification capability** — can the
   notification matrix be operated under
   pressure.
6. **Communication capability** — internal +
   external + customer.
7. **Documentation capability** — evidence
   produced contemporaneously.
8. **Post-incident review capability** — reviews
   produce program improvement.

For each dimension:

- Current state (specific evidence supporting the
  assessment).
- Maturity rating.
- Top gap(s) at this dimension.
- Recommendations for improvement.

### Section 3 — Aldwych-specific concerns (≤ ½ page)

Three specific concerns for Aldwych:

- **Clinical-authority boundary** — the CMO and
  the AI program's response intersect in
  clinical incidents.
- **Patient-affected notifications** —
  notification to patients has clinical-
  communication implications.
- **Regulator landscape** — MHRA + CQC + ICO +
  potentially EU AI Act all simultaneously.

### Section 4 — Overall readiness verdict (≤ ¼ page)

A clear overall verdict:

- Ready / Mostly ready with named gaps / Not
  yet ready with named blockers.
- Defended in one paragraph.
- Specific actions required to reach Ready.

## Constraints

- The assessment must be **honest** —
  acknowledging gaps where they exist. A
  fully-positive readiness assessment for a
  newly-implemented program is implausible.
- Each dimension must include **specific
  evidence** supporting the rating, not just
  assertions.
- The Aldwych-specific concerns must be
  addressed substantively — not just listed.
- The overall verdict must be specific. "Mostly
  ready with caveats" is not specific; "Mostly
  ready; the three named blockers must be
  resolved before classifying the system as
  operational" is.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Method specified | 10% |
| Eight dimensions covered with evidence-based ratings | 35% |
| Aldwych-specific concerns addressed | 20% |
| Overall verdict specific and defended | 20% |
| Honest acknowledgment of gaps | 15% |
