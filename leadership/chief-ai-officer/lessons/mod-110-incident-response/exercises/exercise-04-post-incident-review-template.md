# Exercise 04 — Author a Post-Incident Review Template

**Estimated time**: 3 hours
**Deliverable**: Review template + worked example (≤ 3 pages)

---

## The scenario

You are the CAO at **Northfield Mutual**. The Audit
Committee, after reviewing Northfield's recent
incidents (per mod-107 Ex-04 and mod-110 Ex-01
contexts), has asked you to author a **standard
post-incident review template** that ensures all
material AI incidents receive consistent treatment.

## Your assignment

Produce two artifacts.

### Artifact 1 — The template (≤ 1½ pages)

A template structured per §6.1 of the lecture
notes. The template covers:

1. **Incident summary** — what happened.
2. **Response summary** — what was done.
3. **What worked** — positive findings.
4. **What didn't** — substantive findings.
5. **Root causes** — proximate and systemic per
   §5.2.
6. **Recommendations** — specific, assigned,
   tracked per §6.3.
7. **Status of recommendations** — open / in
   progress / closed.
8. **Sign-off** — review lead, AI Risk Lead,
   AI Risk Council acknowledgment.

For each section, name:

- The fields to populate.
- Guidance to authors (one-sentence prompt).
- An example of what good content looks like.

### Artifact 2 — Worked example (≤ 1½ pages)

Apply the template to a hypothetical incident at
Northfield. Use the bias-incident scenario from
mod-110 Ex-01 or a similar pattern. Fill in every
section as a fully-realised review.

Specifically demonstrate:

- The "what worked" discipline (§6.2) — name at
  least two things that worked.
- The proximate vs. systemic causation
  distinction (§5.3) — name both.
- The recommendation discipline (§6.3) — at least
  3 specific, assigned, tracked recommendations.

## Constraints

- The template must include the "what worked"
  section explicitly. Programs that omit it
  develop the defensive culture §6.2 warns
  against.
- The template must distinguish proximate cause
  from systemic cause as separate fields.
- The recommendation section must include status
  tracking — recommendations without status
  become wallpaper.
- The worked example must include at least one
  recommendation that is *acceptance of a
  documented residual* rather than a remediation
  action. Programs that recommend remediation
  for everything are not credible.
- The combined length is ≤ 3 pages.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Template — eight sections with fields + prompts + examples | 30% |
| What worked discipline | 15% |
| Proximate vs systemic causation distinguished | 15% |
| Recommendation discipline applied | 15% |
| Worked example — fully populated | 15% |
| At least one residual-acceptance recommendation | 10% |
