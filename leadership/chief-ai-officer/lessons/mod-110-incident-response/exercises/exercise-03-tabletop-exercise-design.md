# Exercise 03 — Lead a Tabletop Exercise Design

**Estimated time**: 3 hours
**Deliverable**: Tabletop design + scoring rubric (≤ 3 pages)

---

## The scenario

You are the CAO at **Halverston Capital**. The
Audit Committee has asked that the AI program run
its first cross-functional tabletop exercise.
Participants will include CAO function, CISO,
MRM, GC, CCO, CTO, business-unit heads from all
three LOBs, and an external facilitator.

You have been asked to **author the tabletop
design**. The exercise will run for one half-day.

## Your assignment

Produce a tabletop design covering:

### Section 1 — Goals (≤ ¼ page)

Three specific outcomes the exercise is intended
to produce:

- A specific readiness indicator (e.g., can the
  team make the first-hour decisions within the
  first hour).
- A specific coordination indicator (e.g., do the
  CAO function and CISO coordinate effectively
  on a joint incident).
- A specific learning indicator (the exercise
  surfaces at least one substantive issue).

### Section 2 — The scenario (≤ 1 page)

A realistic AI-incident scenario tailored to
Halverston's multi-LOB structure. The scenario:

- Originates from a *real pattern* (a near-miss
  Halverston had, a peer-institution incident,
  or a documented vendor incident).
- Affects multiple LOBs to test cross-LOB
  coordination.
- Has notification implications (so the
  notification matrix gets exercised).
- Has at least one **ambiguous** classification
  call.

Write the scenario as a 1-page narrative the
facilitator will use to drive the exercise.

### Section 3 — Injects (≤ ¾ page)

The injects (events introduced into the exercise
to drive decisions) over the half-day, at
specific simulated times:

| Simulated time | Inject | Decision required |
|---|---|---|
| T+0 | Initial detection | First-hour decisions |
| T+1h | New information narrows classification | Reclassification decision |
| T+3h | Regulator preliminary inquiry arrives | External coordination |
| T+6h | Containment decision pressure | Containment-posture |
| T+12h | Customer complaints surface | Customer notification |
| ... | ... | ... |

Aim for 6–8 injects spread across the half-day.

### Section 4 — Roles and observers (≤ ¼ page)

Specify:

- Which roles play active in the exercise.
- Which roles observe (typically: an Audit
  Committee member, internal audit, the
  external facilitator).
- The facilitator's role (drive injects;
  maintain time; document decisions; not
  participate substantively).

### Section 5 — Scoring rubric (≤ ½ page)

How the exercise's findings will be scored. Four
dimensions:

- **Decision quality.** Were the right decisions
  made?
- **Decision timing.** Were the decisions made
  in the right window?
- **Coordination.** Did the cross-functional
  team coordinate effectively?
- **Documentation.** Were the decisions
  documented contemporaneously?

Each dimension scored on a 1-5 scale with
specific anchor points.

### Section 6 — Post-exercise review (≤ ¼ page)

Specify:

- The post-exercise hot-wash (30 minutes
  immediately after).
- The written exercise report (within 10
  business days).
- Recommendations from the exercise routed into
  the program improvement backlog.

## Constraints

- The scenario must be **realistic** —
  recognisable as a plausible Halverston incident.
- The injects must include **at least one
  ambiguous** classification call.
- The scoring rubric must include the
  **documentation** dimension — programs that
  ignore documentation in tabletops produce
  responses that don't survive audit.
- The post-exercise review must produce
  *recommendations*, not just findings.
- The exercise must not exceed half a day in real
  time.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Goals — three specific indicators | 15% |
| Scenario — realistic, multi-LOB, ambiguous | 25% |
| Injects — 6–8 with decision triggers | 25% |
| Roles and observers — specific | 10% |
| Scoring rubric — four dimensions | 15% |
| Post-exercise review specified | 10% |
