# Module 104 — Resources

Annotated reading list for Model Risk Management.

## Tier 1 — Authoritative (read these)

| Source | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| [OCC/FRB SR 11-7](https://www.federalreserve.gov/supervisionreg/srletters/sr1107.htm) | The source. Twenty-one pages. Read it before anything else. The lecture notes summarise; the source is what matters. |
| [FRB SR 22-6](https://www.federalreserve.gov/supervisionreg/srletters/sr2206.htm) | Current Federal Reserve expectations on AI/ML model validation; pairs with SR 11-7 |
| [EBA Guidelines on Model Risk Management (2023)](https://www.eba.europa.eu/) | European parallel to SR 11-7; useful for comparative reading |
| [OCC Comptroller's Handbook on Model Risk Management](https://www.occ.gov/publications-and-resources/publications/comptrollers-handbook/) | Examiner-side reference; useful for understanding how SR 11-7 gets applied in supervision |

## Tier 2 — Authoritative, sector-specific analogs

| Source | Sector | Use |
|---|---|---|
| [FDA Predetermined Change Control Plan guidance](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/marketing-submission-recommendations-predetermined-change-control-plan-artificial-intelligence) | Healthcare | Analog for continuous-learning systems |
| [NAIC Model Law on AI Systems](https://content.naic.org/) | Insurance | State-adopted MRM-like discipline for insurance |
| [NIST AI RMF Playbook MANAGE sub-functions](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework) | Cross-sector | Pairs MRM disciplines with the NIST framework |
| [Canadian Directive on Automated Decision-Making](https://www.tbs-sct.canada.ca/) | Public sector | The clearest public-sector analog to SR 11-7 |

## Tier 3 — Foundational

| Source | Use |
|---|---|
| [ISO/IEC 23894 §7 (Risk treatment)](https://www.iso.org/standard/77304.html) | Risk-treatment discipline; pairs with mod-103 §5 |
| [COSO ERM — Applying ERM to AI (2024)](https://www.coso.org/Pages/erm.aspx) | Boards reading MRM reports come from this background |
| mod-101 §3 (Three Lines of Defense) + §4 (CAO peer-role boundaries) | Required context for §5 of this module's lecture notes |
| mod-103 §6 (GOVERN continuously) | The reporting / cadence discipline that MRM must integrate with |

## Tier 4 — Practitioner references (range, not template)

| Source | Pattern illustrated |
|---|---|
| Public-domain commentary from large-bank MRM functions (RBC, JPMorgan, Goldman) | One pattern for mature MRM operating at scale |
| [Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-responsible-scaling-policy) | A non-banking adaptation of risk-tiered model governance |
| [Microsoft Responsible AI Standard v2](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/ai/responsible-ai) | Cross-portfolio model governance at a hyperscaler |
