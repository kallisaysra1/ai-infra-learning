# Exercise 01 — Compare Three Ethics Frameworks and Find the Operational Disagreement

**Estimated time**: 3 hours
**Deliverable**: A comparison table + a one-page
operational-disagreement memo

---

## Why this exercise exists

§2.2 of the lecture notes named the convergence problem:
principles documents agree at the principle level and
diverge at the operationalization level. This exercise
makes you do the actual work of finding the divergence
— which is where the CAO's framing-principle-set choice
actually matters.

## Your assignment

Compare three of the following frameworks on the
operational implications of their stated principles:

1. OECD AI Principles (2024 update)
2. UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of AI (2021)
3. IEEE 7000 series (focus on 7000, 7001, 7003)
4. EU HLEG Ethics Guidelines for Trustworthy AI
5. Microsoft Responsible AI Standard v2 (practitioner
   reference)

You **must** include OECD as one of the three. The other
two are your choice. (Microsoft RAI is practitioner-only;
including it means you compare against an internal
operationalization, which is a different exercise.)

### Artifact 1 — Comparison table

A table that compares the three frameworks on **five
operational dimensions**. Suggested dimensions (pick at
least three from these, plus add two of your own):

- **Bias and fairness** — what specifically must a
  fair AI system do, per each framework?
- **Transparency** — what specifically must be
  transparent, to whom?
- **Human oversight** — what specifically constitutes
  adequate oversight?
- **Privacy** — what specifically must a system protect?
- **Accountability** — who specifically is responsible
  when things go wrong?
- **Safety / robustness** — what specifically must be
  demonstrated?
- **Beneficence / well-being** — what specifically does
  "do good" require?

For each cell, give a *specific operational claim* the
framework would support, with a one-sentence quote or
paraphrase. Mark cells with `~` where the framework
gives only a general principle without operational
content; mark cells with `—` where the framework does
not address the dimension at all.

### Artifact 2 — Operational-disagreement memo (1 page)

A memo addressing:

1. **The two clearest operational disagreements** you
   found across the three frameworks. Be specific: not
   "they disagree on bias" but "Framework A operationalises
   bias as equalized odds, while Framework B operationalises
   it as procedural-fairness, leading to different
   validation tests."

2. **The two clearest operational *agreements*.** Areas
   where the frameworks converge specifically enough
   to inform a program.

3. **Recommendation: which of the three would you adopt
   as the program's framing-principle anchor**, and why?
   Or: would you adopt a *combination*? Defend the
   choice.

4. **What you would *not* adopt** — at least one
   element of one framework you would reject, and the
   reasoning.

## Constraints

- The disagreements must be **operational**, not
  rhetorical. "They use different words for fairness"
  is not an operational disagreement. "They require
  different validation tests" is.
- The comparison table must include **specific quotes
  or paraphrases** with locator citations (section,
  article, principle number).
- The recommendation must be **defensible** to both an
  AI ethics academic and a CAO peer. A choice neither
  would accept is not viable.
- You must name **what you would not adopt**. A program
  that adopts everything from every framework is not a
  program; it is a collage.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Comparison table — five dimensions × three frameworks, specific operational content | 25% |
| Citations — specific locators in each cell | 10% |
| Disagreements — two clear operational disagreements identified | 25% |
| Agreements — two clear operational agreements identified | 15% |
| Recommendation — defensible, includes reasoning | 15% |
| What you would not adopt — present, substantive | 10% |
