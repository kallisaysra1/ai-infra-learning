# Deliverable 07 — Article 73 Serious Incident Reporting Procedure

**Target:** Days 41-48. **Length:** ≤ 4 pages.
**Modules:** mod-102 §2.7; mod-107 §6 (incident
classification); mod-110 (entire — incident
response).

## What this deliverable is

The serious-incident reporting procedure per
Art. 73 for KH-AI-027. Must be in place
*before* the system is placed on the market.
Defines what constitutes a serious incident,
the reporting timeline, the procedure for
preparing and submitting the report, the
follow-up obligations.

## What it should contain

1. **Serious incident definition** for
   KH-AI-027 specifically. Per Art. 73(1),
   any incident or malfunctioning that leads
   to or could lead to:
   - Death of a person.
   - Serious damage to a person's health.
   - Serious and irreversible disruption of
     critical infrastructure.
   - Infringement of obligations under EU
     law intended to protect fundamental
     rights.
   - Serious damage to property or
     environment.
   For KH-AI-027, the first and second
   bullets are the operationally relevant
   triggers; the others largely don't apply
   to a diabetes-screening tool.
2. **Reporting timelines:**
   - 2 days for fundamental-rights incidents
     (e.g., systematic discriminatory bias
     producing differential clinical
     outcomes).
   - 15 days for other serious incidents.
   - Immediate for catastrophic infrastructure
     events (not applicable to this system).
3. **Recipient and channel.** National
   competent authority per the Member State
   where KH-AI-027 is placed on the market;
   identification of the specific authority
   for each Member State Kerridge will
   launch in.
4. **Detection.** How serious incidents are
   detected — PMM (Deliverable 06); user-
   reported events; ophthalmologist-reported
   feedback; clinical-outcome data; bias-
   monitoring threshold crossings.
5. **Classification.** Per the mod-107 §6
   taxonomy: AI-program, security, or joint.
   Initial classification within 4 hours of
   detection.
6. **Lead and escalation.** Single named
   lead pattern per mod-110 §1. For
   KH-AI-027: clinical safety officer is the
   first responder; CAO function leads on
   Art. 73 specifically; CMO designee on
   clinical communications.
7. **Investigation discipline.** Per mod-110
   §5 — proximate vs. systemic causation;
   blame-free NTSB-style framing.
8. **Reporting structure** — what gets
   reported; what the report format is;
   approvals required before transmission.
9. **Follow-up.** Per Art. 73(7) —
   continued investigation; cooperation with
   the authority; corrective actions.
10. **Coordination with other reporting
    regimes** — GDPR Arts 33-34 if
    personal data involved; ISO 13485
    incident reporting; FDA MDR; UK MHRA
    where applicable.
11. **Tabletop exercise** committed for
    the procedure prior to launch.

## Constraints

- The procedure must be **operational** — a
  Kerridge employee could read it and know
  what to do during an incident.
- Reporting timelines must cite Art. 73
  specifically.
- Member-State-specific competent
  authorities must be **identified by name**
  for at least the launch Member States.
- The cross-regime coordination must be
  substantive — Art. 73 is not the only
  obligation; the procedure addresses how
  Kerridge handles overlapping requirements.
