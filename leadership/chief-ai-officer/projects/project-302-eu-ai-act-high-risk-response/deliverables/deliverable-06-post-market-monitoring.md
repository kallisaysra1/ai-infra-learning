# Deliverable 06 — Article 72 Post-Market Monitoring System

**Target:** Days 33-40. **Length:** 4-5 pages.
**Modules:** mod-102 §2.6; mod-109 §3
(continuous evidence); mod-110 §1-2.

## What this deliverable is

The post-market monitoring system as a
**separable artifact** per Art. 72. This is the
artifact that distinguishes EU AI Act from NIST
AI RMF — Art. 72 requires the PMM exist as a
defined system, not merely as an aggregate of
ongoing activities. mod-102 §2.6 named this; this
deliverable produces it.

## What it should contain

1. **PMM scope and purpose.** What KH-AI-027's
   PMM monitors; how it differs from the
   broader Kerridge Healthcare quality
   management system (which is ISO 13485-
   based).
2. **What is monitored.** Performance metrics;
   bias-stratified subgroup performance
   (per Deliverable 02 RMS treatment plans);
   data drift; clinical outcomes (where
   available); user-reported issues;
   incidents.
3. **How monitoring operates.** Data
   collection from deployed devices; secure
   transmission to Kerridge Healthcare PMM
   infrastructure; aggregation; analysis.
4. **Cadence.** Per-metric review cadence
   per mod-109 Ex-02 pattern (operating,
   steward, audit).
5. **Thresholds and triggers.** Per-metric
   thresholds that trigger investigation;
   thresholds that trigger Art. 73 incident
   reporting; thresholds that trigger
   product hold or recall.
6. **Roles.** Who operates the PMM
   day-to-day; who reviews; who decides on
   triggered actions; how authorities
   coordinate.
7. **Documentation and audit trail.** Per
   mod-108 evidence-layer integration; PMM
   outputs feed the audit ledger.
8. **Integration with other monitoring
   systems.** ISO 13485 quality monitoring
   (existing); FDA post-market surveillance
   (parallel); customer-deployment
   monitoring (planned).

## Constraints

- The PMM must be **a separable artifact** —
  not just "we will monitor". The
  deliverable describes the actual system
  Kerridge will operate.
- Thresholds must be **specific** —
  numerical or categorical, not "as
  appropriate".
- The Art. 73 trigger thresholds must
  cross-reference the incident-reporting
  procedure in Deliverable 07.
- Integration with the existing ISO 13485
  quality system must be substantive —
  PMM doesn't replicate existing monitoring;
  it adds AI-specific dimensions.
