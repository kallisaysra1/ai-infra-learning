# Synthesis Memo — Conformity Submission Preparation

**Target:** Days 51-60. **Length:** ≤ 3 pages.

## What this memo is

The synthesis at the end of the 60-day arc. Goes
to the Kerridge Healthcare CEO, the Kerridge
Industries board, and the Notified Body
engagement file. Internally, this is what the
CAO writes to themselves at the conclusion of
the conformity-preparation work.

## What it should contain

1. **What was produced.** The seven
   deliverables that comprise the EU AI Act
   conformity package, in summary.
2. **What is the Notified Body about to
   see.** A reading guide. What the Notified
   Body will likely focus on; what we expect
   them to ask; how Kerridge will respond.
3. **What we got wrong over the 60 days.**
   Per mod-112 §6.3 honesty discipline.
   At least 2 substantive items.
4. **What's fragile in the conformity
   package.** Per mod-112 §6.3 — what would
   need addressing if the Notified Body
   pressed hardest.
5. **The launch implication.** Assuming
   Notified Body engagement goes per plan,
   the path to EU launch. Assuming it
   doesn't, the contingency plan.
6. **The handoff condition** — if the CAO
   were unavailable for the Notified Body
   engagement, how the package would be
   presented by someone else.

## Constraints

- The "what we got wrong" must be
  substantive. Programs that produce
  fully-positive conformity packages without
  acknowledged compromises in 60 days are
  not credible.
- The fragility section must name **specific**
  fragilities, not generic concerns.
- The launch-implication framing must be
  honest about timeline risk.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Package summary | 15% |
| Notified Body reading guide | 20% |
| What we got wrong — substantive | 25% |
| Fragility — specific | 20% |
| Launch implication — honest | 15% |
| Handoff condition addressed | 5% |
