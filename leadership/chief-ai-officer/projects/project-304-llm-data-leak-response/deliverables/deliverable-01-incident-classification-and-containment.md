# Deliverable 01 — Incident Classification and Containment

**Target:** Days 0-2. **Length:** Working
document; ≤ 4 pages.

## What this deliverable is

The first artifact of the incident response.
Classifies the incident severity, declares
the incident formally, names roles, defines
initial containment. Goes to the executive
team and the Board for situational
awareness.

## What it should contain

1. **Incident declaration.** Formal
   declaration with timestamp; classification
   tier; named incident commander; named
   teams.
2. **Initial scope statement.** What is
   known; what is not yet known; what is
   being investigated.
3. **Containment actions.** What has been
   done to limit blast radius. What is
   in-flight. Specific.
4. **Classification reasoning.** Why this
   tier and not the adjacent tiers.
5. **Initial timeline.** Day-by-day plan for
   the first week.
6. **Stakeholder communication plan
   (internal).** Who gets what information
   when. Executive team, Board, employee
   communication.
7. **What we will NOT do yet.** Customer
   notification timing; press posture;
   regulatory notification — these are
   covered in subsequent deliverables.
   Naming the deferrals prevents premature
   action.

## Constraints

- Classification must be defensible. Over-
  classification creates panic; under-
  classification creates exposure when
  scope expands.
- Containment must be specific and
  verifiable. "We are investigating" is
  not containment.
- The CAO × CISO boundary applies; mod-107
  pattern. Be specific about ownership.
- Privilege considerations apply throughout
  (General Counsel involvement matters).

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Classification defensible | 20% |
| Containment specific | 25% |
| Roles named (CAO×CISO boundary) | 15% |
| Initial timeline realistic | 15% |
| Internal comms plan | 15% |
| What-we-will-NOT-do yet | 10% |
