# Deliverable 03 — Customer Notification Strategy and Materials

**Target:** Days 5-10. **Length:** Strategy
memo + notification letter templates +
customer-conversation playbook.

## What this deliverable is

The customer notification artifact. Strategy
memo to the executive team; specific
notification letters for affected customers
(tiered by exposure level); a playbook for
Customer Success and Sales conversations
during the notification window. Goes to
General Counsel for review; CEO for
approval; Customer Success for execution.

## What it should contain

### Strategy memo

1. **Notification tiers.** Different
   customer cohorts get different
   notifications:
   - High-exposure (confirmed disclosure):
     direct CISO-to-CISO + written
     notification + executive call.
   - Medium-exposure (potential disclosure
     within scope window): direct
     notification with specific exposure
     details.
   - Low-exposure (not affected per
     analysis): proactive notification
     describing the incident and
     confirming non-exposure (this is
     trust-preserving).
2. **Sequencing.** Order of notification.
   High-exposure customers first; press-
   risk customers second; full notification
   cohort thereafter.
3. **Timing.** Specific dates per cohort.
   Aligned with contractual obligations
   and regulatory windows (Deliverable 04).
4. **Channel.** Written + verbal. Who
   leads the verbal conversation.

### Notification letters

5. **High-exposure letter.** Specific to
   the customer; identifies what data was
   affected; what Northrise has done;
   what Northrise will do; what the
   customer should do.
6. **Medium-exposure letter.** Less
   specific; identifies the potential
   exposure window and data classes.
7. **Low-exposure letter.** Acknowledges
   the incident; confirms non-exposure
   per Northrise's analysis; describes
   the remediation; preserves trust.

### Customer-conversation playbook

8. **Pre-call preparation.** What CS
   should know about each customer before
   making contact.
9. **Talking points and FAQ.** Specific
   questions customers will ask; approved
   answers.
10. **Escalation triggers.** When CS
    pulls in CAO, CEO, GC.
11. **Documentation.** Each customer
    conversation logged.

## Constraints

- Customer notifications must be specific
  enough to be useful and general enough
  to preserve privilege.
- Contractual obligations vary by customer.
  Notifications must meet the most
  stringent obligation per cohort.
- Tone discipline. Defensive tone produces
  customer escalation. Over-apologetic
  tone produces legal exposure. The right
  posture is direct, accountable,
  forward-looking.
- The low-exposure proactive notification
  is a judgment call. Some companies
  choose to notify only confirmed-
  affected customers. Northrise's choice
  to proactively notify all customers
  reflects the trust posture; the strategy
  memo names this choice and reasoning.
