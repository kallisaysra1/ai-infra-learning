# Synthesis Memo — 30-day Incident Closeout

**Target:** Day 30. **Length:** ≤ 3 pages.

## What this memo is

The 30-day closeout. Goes to the CEO, the
Board, the executive team. Internally, what
the CAO writes to themselves at the
conclusion of the incident response. Distinct
from the post-mortem (Deliverable 06) — the
post-mortem is the technical and process
account; the synthesis is the
organisational and strategic account.

## What it should contain

1. **30-day outcome.** What state Northrise
   is in today vs. Day 0.
2. **Customer-relationship state.** Where
   each affected customer cohort stands.
   Where the high-exposure customers
   stand specifically.
3. **What the response cost.** Direct
   costs (response staffing, customer
   credits if any, third-party assurance,
   legal). Indirect costs (engineering
   capacity diverted; product roadmap
   slip; sales-pipeline impact).
4. **What we got wrong over 30 days.**
   Per mod-112 §6.3. At least 3
   substantive items.
5. **What's fragile going forward.**
   Specific fragilities.
6. **What this changes about Northrise.**
   Structural changes in how Northrise
   builds, ships, and operates
   GuardianGPT. Some captured in
   Deliverable 05; some broader.
7. **The board's outstanding questions.**
   What the Board will want to know in
   Q1, Q2.
8. **The CAO's posture going forward.**
   How this incident reframes the CAO's
   work for the next 6-12 months.

## Constraints

- Direct and quantitative where possible.
- "What we got wrong" must be substantive.
- "What's fragile" must be specific.
- Honest about cost.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| 30-day outcome summary | 15% |
| Customer-relationship state honest | 15% |
| Response cost quantitative | 15% |
| What we got wrong substantive | 25% |
| Fragility specific | 15% |
| Structural changes named | 10% |
| CAO posture forward | 5% |
