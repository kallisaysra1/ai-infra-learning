# Deliverable 04 — Regulatory Notification Strategy

**Target:** Days 3-15. **Length:** Strategy
memo + per-regime notification frameworks.

## What this deliverable is

The regulatory notification artifact. Maps
Northrise's specific incident facts onto
each applicable regulatory regime; produces
notification timelines and content
frameworks per regime. Goes to General
Counsel for legal review; Compliance for
execution; CEO and Board for awareness.

## What it should contain

### Regulatory regime mapping

1. **GDPR (Art. 33 and Art. 34).** 72-hour
   authority notification; data subject
   notification when high risk. For
   Northrise: applies to EU customers and
   their EU-resident data subjects.
2. **HIPAA (Business Associate breach
   notification).** Notification to
   covered-entity customers within
   stipulated window; covered entities
   then notify individuals.
3. **GLBA / state breach laws.**
   Financial-services-related customer
   data exposure triggers state-specific
   notification regimes. Patchwork
   coverage; varies by state.
4. **SEC and Northrise's investor
   reporting.** Material-incident
   disclosure considerations for pre-IPO
   Northrise; coordinated with General
   Counsel and CFO.
5. **Sector-specific regimes.** NYDFS Part
   500 for relevant financial-services
   customers; other state-specific
   regulations.
6. **EU AI Act.** GuardianGPT is not
   itself a high-risk AI system per the
   AI Act (it is a general-purpose
   assistant); incident does not trigger
   Art. 73 directly. However, Northrise's
   foundation model use and customer
   exposure require analysis.

### Per-regime frameworks

For each applicable regime:

7. **Trigger analysis.** Does Northrise
   have to notify? When?
8. **Recipient analysis.** Which
   authority(ies)? Per Member State in
   GDPR.
9. **Content framework.** What the
   notification must contain.
10. **Timing.** Specific dates with
    justification.
11. **Coordination with customer
    notification.** Many regimes flow
    through customer (Northrise's
    customers are the data controllers
    for their data; they then notify
    their authorities). Northrise's
    obligation is to enable the
    customer's compliance.

### Investor / SEC framing

12. **Materiality assessment.** Is the
    incident material to Northrise as a
    pre-IPO company?
13. **Investor communication strategy.**
    What to communicate to existing
    investors; timing; channel.
14. **S-1 implications.** If Northrise is
    in S-1 preparation, how this incident
    appears in registration disclosures.

## Constraints

- Most notification timelines run from
  "awareness" of the incident. Northrise
  needs a defensible position on when
  awareness was achieved.
- Many regimes flow through Northrise's
  customers (they are data controllers).
  Northrise's obligation is processor-side
  notification to customer to enable
  customer's compliance. Different from
  direct authority notification.
- The press posture interacts with
  regulatory notifications. Coordinated
  timing matters.
- General Counsel privilege protections
  apply.
