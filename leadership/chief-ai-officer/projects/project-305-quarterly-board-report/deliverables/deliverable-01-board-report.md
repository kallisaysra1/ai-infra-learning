# Deliverable 01 — Q2 Quarterly Board AI Risk Report

**Target length:** 10-15 pages.

## What this deliverable is

The Q2 quarterly AI risk report to the full
Halverston Board. Substantive document,
read by directors who collectively span
AI-fluent and AI-learning. Sets the
template for future quarterly Board
reviews.

## What it should contain

Suggested structure (you may revise with
reasoning):

### Section 1 — Executive summary

≤ 1 page. The director who reads only this
page should understand: state of AI risk
at Halverston; what changed this quarter;
top three things to attend to going
forward.

### Section 2 — State of AI at Halverston

What's in production, in what business
lines, in what tiers. Quantitative
scorecard. Concise enough that the
Board can absorb the picture without
becoming a model census.

### Section 3 — Quarter events of note

The six Q2 events, framed for Board
audience. Each event:
- What happened.
- What it tells us.
- What changed as a result.

Honest framing. Not "everything is
fine"; not "everything is on fire."

### Section 4 — AI risk register update

The current top risks at Halverston,
named, with assessment of trend
(rising, stable, falling). Specific
attention to risks the Risk Committee
chair has historically followed.

### Section 5 — Regulatory horizon

What's coming. EU AI Act high-risk
work; SEC AI rulemaking; CFPB AI
guidance; state-level regulation.
Specific to Halverston's exposure
profile.

### Section 6 — Customer and product
implications

(Per lead independent director's ask.)
What the AI risk picture means for
Halverston's customers, products,
and competitive position. Includes:
the LLM agent adoption pattern; the
vendor-LLM incident's customer
implications; the EU AI Act exposure
implications.

### Section 7 — What we are worrying
about going forward

(Per independent chair's ask.) The 3-5
forward-looking risks the CAO is most
attentive to. Specific. Honest.

### Section 8 — Q3 priorities

Resourcing implications. Specific
ask of the Board for direction,
authority, or resources.

### Section 9 — Appendices index

Pointer to the supporting
appendices (Deliverable 02).

## Constraints

- Audience discipline. AI-fluent and
  AI-learning directors both serve.
  Specific jargon explained or
  avoided.
- Honesty discipline. The
  hallucination, the alpha pause, the
  vendor incident, the EU AI Act
  surprise, the CFPB letter all
  warrant honest treatment.
- Metrics-over-narrative for the Risk
  Committee chair. Forward-looking
  for the independent chair. Customer-
  product for the lead independent.
  All three Board-member asks served.
- Template-quality. This report
  defines the structure for future
  quarters; design choices matter.
- Reading time ≤ 45 minutes.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Executive summary serves the brief-read director | 15% |
| State-of-AI scorecard quantitative | 10% |
| Quarter events honestly framed | 20% |
| Forward-looking risk section substantive | 20% |
| Customer/product implications addressed | 10% |
| Regulatory horizon specific | 10% |
| Q3 priorities and asks clear | 10% |
| Template-quality (sets the standing pattern) | 5% |
