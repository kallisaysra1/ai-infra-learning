# Scenario — Halverston Capital, Q2 Board AI Risk Report

## Halverston Capital

Halverston is a $40B asset manager, 850
employees, headquartered in Boston with
offices in London, Singapore, New York.
Lines of business: institutional asset
management; private wealth; alternatives
(real estate, infrastructure, private
equity fund-of-funds). Regulated by SEC,
FCA, MAS depending on entity.

## You

You are CAO at Halverston, reporting to
the CEO with dotted-line accountability to
the Risk Committee. You have been in role
18 months. You report quarterly to the
Risk Committee in person and produce a
written quarterly report.

This Q2 report (for the quarter ending
June 30) is your fifth quarterly report.
It is the **first** that the full Board
will review — Halverston's January
governance refresh elevated AI risk to
standing Board agenda.

## The state of AI at Halverston

(Brief — full quantitative picture lives
in the appendices for the deliverable.)

- ~78 AI/ML models in production or in
  scope, per the model inventory.
- AI use cases: portfolio analytics,
  risk modeling, ESG signal
  construction, alpha-generation signals,
  CRM intelligence, compliance
  monitoring, KYC/AML augmentation,
  client-portal personalization,
  internal productivity (LLM agents for
  research support).
- AI governance: AI Risk Council
  established 14 months ago; MRM
  extension to AI/ML complete 8 months
  ago; quarterly Risk Committee report
  in place; AI Acceptable Use Policy
  v3.1.

## Q2 events of note

The quarter included substantive AI-
related activity:

1. **An LLM agent fine-tuned for
   research support went live in April
   for the institutional asset
   management team.** Adoption is
   strong (~80% of analysts using
   weekly). One material complaint:
   the LLM hallucinated a citation in
   an investment-committee memo; the
   senior analyst caught it before
   distribution; documented for
   learning.
2. **An alpha-generation signal model
   went through significant
   re-development.** Production
   monitoring detected calibration
   drift in February; model was
   paused; rebuilt with updated
   training data; re-deployed in May
   under MRM validation. The pause-
   to-redeploy arc is the right
   discipline; the pre-pause
   monitoring discipline is the
   question.
3. **A vendor LLM used for client-
   facing portfolio explanations
   (vendor: a specific named provider;
   the system summarizes portfolio
   positions for client review)** had
   a vendor-side incident in May: the
   vendor disclosed a security
   incident affecting an upstream
   foundation-model dependency. No
   Halverston customer data was
   confirmed affected. Halverston
   evaluated; continued use under
   enhanced monitoring; documented.
4. **EU AI Act high-risk classification
   work.** The Q2 work surfaced that
   two Halverston systems are
   plausibly high-risk under the EU
   AI Act: a credit-related decision
   support system (Annex III financial)
   and an employee performance
   support model (Annex III employment).
   Classification determinations
   pending; conformity work in early
   planning.
5. **One CFPB inquiry letter** received
   in June regarding alpha-generation
   models' interaction with retail
   wealth clients. Routine; under
   response.
6. **MRM validator hiring.** Two new
   senior AI/ML-capable validators
   started in April. Validation
   backlog reduced from 14 to 6 models.

## Board context

The Board includes:
- Independent chair (former large-bank
  CRO; reads AI risk through risk-
  governance lens).
- Lead independent director (former
  tech-company CEO; pushes for
  product-and-customer framing).
- Risk Committee chair (former
  insurance CFO; reads quantitatively).
- 7 other directors with varied
  backgrounds (asset management,
  legal, academic, fintech).

The Board has limited collective AI
expertise. Some directors will be
catching up on technical concepts. The
report needs to serve both AI-fluent and
AI-learning readers.

## Constraints

- Report length: ~10-15 pages plus
  supporting appendices. Board read
  time ≤ 45 minutes.
- The Risk Committee already sees
  detailed reports; the full Board
  needs the digestible version with
  appropriate context.
- The first-Board-review status means
  this report sets the template.
  Future quarterly reports will
  follow this pattern.
- Honesty discipline applies. The
  hallucination incident, the alpha
  signal pause, the vendor incident,
  the EU AI Act surprise, and the
  CFPB letter all warrant honest
  treatment.
- The Risk Committee chair has asked
  for "metrics over narrative."
  Quantitative substance matters.
- The independent chair has asked for
  "what should we be worrying about?"
  Forward-looking risk identification
  matters.
- The lead independent director has
  asked for "what does this mean for
  customers and products?"
  Customer-and-product framing
  matters.

## You have access to

- All prior modules (101-112).
- The 4 prior quarterly reports.
- Halverston's AI inventory, risk
  register, monitoring dashboards.
- The MRM team and their validation
  pipeline.
- The general counsel and compliance
  teams.

## What the work is

Produce the Q2 quarterly Board report
(the main deliverable, ~10-15 pages)
plus supporting appendices (the data
that backs the report; the technical
detail that some directors will want;
the regulatory horizon detail). The
report is both a Q2 deliverable AND
the template for future quarterly
Board reviews.
