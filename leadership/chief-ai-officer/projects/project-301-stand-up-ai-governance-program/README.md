# Capstone 301 — Stand Up an AI Governance Program for a 250-Person AI SaaS

> First capstone of the Chief AI Officer track.
> Integrates ~7 foundational modules into a single
> multi-week project simulating the first 90 days
> as the inaugural CAO at a fast-growing AI-native
> company.

## What this capstone is for

The foundational modules taught the disciplines one
at a time. This capstone exercises them in
combination, under realistic stakes, against a
realistic timeline. Working through it produces a
portfolio of artifacts the reader could plausibly
present to a Board, a regulator, or a peer CAO as
evidence of operational readiness.

## Estimated time

**20–30 hours.** Distributed across multiple
sessions; the capstone is structured to support
working it across several weeks.

## What you will produce

Seven substantial deliverables across the 90-day
arc:

| # | Deliverable | Integrates |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | Program charter | mod-101 |
| 02 | AI risk taxonomy | mod-103 |
| 03 | Year-1 operating plan | mod-112 |
| 04 | AI risk appetite statement | mod-111 |
| 05 | Trust architecture build/buy decision | mod-106 |
| 06 | Compliance operations baseline | mod-109 |
| 07 | First quarterly board report | mod-111 |

Plus a **synthesis memo** at the end that ties
the seven together and articulates the trade-offs
made.

## The scenario

See [`SCENARIO.md`](./SCENARIO.md) for the full
context. In summary:

You have been appointed the inaugural Chief AI
Officer at **Northrise AI**, a 250-person AI-
native SaaS providing AI tooling (agent
infrastructure + LLM-powered analytics) to
mid-market enterprises. Northrise has 800+
customers across financial services, healthcare,
and professional services. Series C, $80M ARR,
growth-focused.

You start on day 1. You have 90 days to produce
the foundation of an AI governance program. The
board has scheduled a Risk Committee review at
day 90 to receive your first quarterly report.

## How to work the capstone

The deliverables are designed to be worked **in
order** (each builds on the prior) but can be
revisited as the project develops. A working
pattern:

- **Days 1–10:** Read the scenario; draft the
  program charter (Deliverable 01); convene the
  AI Risk Council; orient with stakeholders.
- **Days 11–25:** Author the AI risk taxonomy
  (02); use it to scope the year-1 operating
  plan (03).
- **Days 26–45:** Draft the AI risk appetite
  statement (04); circulate for review.
- **Days 46–60:** Make the trust architecture
  build/buy decision (05); document.
- **Days 61–75:** Establish the compliance
  operations baseline (06); first control map.
- **Days 76–90:** Author the first quarterly
  board report (07); prepare for the Risk
  Committee.
- **Day 90:** Synthesis memo.

## Deliverable structure

Each of the seven deliverables has its own file
in [`deliverables/`](./deliverables/) with:

- The specific deliverable scope.
- Required content.
- Constraints and discipline applied.
- A rubric for self-evaluation.
- Pointers to relevant foundational modules.

## How to evaluate yourself

A reader who works through this capstone
seriously will produce ~30 pages of substantive
output. The peer-CAO honesty test from mod-111
§6.3 applies: would you be comfortable showing
your output to a peer CAO at a similar
institution?

Reference solutions in the paired solutions repo
provide one defensible approach. They are not the
only correct approach. The reasoning notes in
each reference solution discuss where the
solution could plausibly have gone differently.
