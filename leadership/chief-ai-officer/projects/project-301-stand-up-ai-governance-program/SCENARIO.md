# Capstone 301 — Scenario

## Northrise AI

You have been appointed the inaugural Chief AI
Officer at **Northrise AI**, effective [start
date]. Today is your day one.

### About Northrise

- **Founded:** 2021. AI-native from day one.
- **Headcount:** 250 employees. Engineering
  ~120, Product ~25, Sales ~40, Customer
  Success ~30, G&A ~25, Leadership ~10.
- **Funding:** Series C closed 6 months ago.
  $180M raised total; $80M ARR; growth target
  $150M ARR by year-end.
- **Headquarters:** San Francisco. Significant
  presence in New York and Dublin (the Dublin
  office handles EU operations).
- **Customers:** ~800 paying customers across
  financial services, healthcare, professional
  services, and technology. Top 20 customers
  account for ~60% of ARR; long-tail customers
  the remainder.
- **Products:**
  - **Northrise Agent Platform** — AI agent
    infrastructure for enterprise customers.
    Customer-deployed agents that integrate with
    customer-internal systems. The flagship
    product.
  - **Northrise Analytics** — LLM-powered
    analytics platform for unstructured business
    data. Used by mid-market firms for things
    like contract review, customer-support
    summarisation, and competitive intelligence.
  - **Northrise Studio** — early-stage
    developer tools for building AI applications
    on Northrise infrastructure.

### Why Northrise hired you

Three factors converged:

1. **Customer pressure.** Large enterprise
   customers (the top 20) have been increasingly
   asking about Northrise's AI governance
   posture. Several have made formal requests
   for SOC 2 + ISO 42001 + AI Bill of Materials.
   The sales team is losing time fielding these
   questions.
2. **Regulatory exposure.** Northrise has EU-
   resident customers using the products to
   process EU-resident data. Some customer
   deployments would be high-risk under the EU
   AI Act. Northrise has not yet operationalised
   any EU AI Act response.
3. **Series D pressure.** The Series C round
   was straightforward. The Series D
   conversations starting in 6 months will not
   be — investors are increasingly diligenced
   on AI governance, and Northrise's posture
   today is insufficient for the diligence
   most institutional investors will run.

### Who you report to

- **Solid line:** CEO. (Northrise does not
  have a CRO; the CEO has indicated they
  would consider it for year-2.)
- **Dotted lines:** General Counsel (for
  regulatory engagement coordination); CFO
  (for budget).
- **Peer relationships:** CTO (manages
  product engineering); VP Customer Success;
  Head of Security (reports to CTO; equivalent
  to CISO at larger firms).

### Your authority

Established by your appointment:

- You **report directly to the CEO**.
- You have a **first-year budget of $4M**
  (your personal $X plus $X for hiring 4-5
  roles plus $X for vendor / tooling).
- You have **convening authority** across
  engineering, product, customer success, and
  legal.
- You **lack** the authority to single-
  handedly stop a product launch or feature
  deployment. To block a launch, you would need
  to bring the case to the CEO.
- You **lack** direct enforcement authority
  over customer-deployed agents (which run on
  customer infrastructure with customer
  configuration).

### What you don't have on day 1

- No AI risk taxonomy.
- No AI risk appetite statement.
- No AI Review Board or AI Risk Council.
- No formal incident classification taxonomy.
- No documented control catalog.
- No audit ledger.
- No documented trust architecture for the
  agent platform.
- No formal compliance posture for EU AI Act,
  state AI regulations, or sector-specific AI
  rules.

### What you do have on day 1

- A **product** (the Agent Platform) with
  active customer deployments and an
  engineering team that has been pragmatic
  about security and reliability without
  having governance discipline.
- **Existing security infrastructure** — SOC 2
  Type 1 was achieved 8 months ago; SOC 2 Type
  2 is in progress. Engineering has an
  existing Identity and Access Management
  stack and audit logging at the
  infrastructure level.
- **A CEO and Board who hired you for this** —
  the appointment was substantive, not a
  marketing move. They will support you;
  they will also expect substance.
- **Existing customers who have already asked
  the right questions** — the AI governance
  posture you build will be tested by these
  customers in their renewal cycles.

### The 90-day arc

Your appointment letter committed to a 90-day
plan. The Board has scheduled a Risk Committee
review at day 90. You need a real program with
real artifacts by then — not slides about a
program.

The seven deliverables in this capstone are
calibrated to what 90 days of substantive work
can plausibly produce. You will not have a
mature program at day 90. You should have
authoritative foundation.

### What success looks like

A reader who completes this capstone seriously
will produce artifacts that:

- A CEO can take to the Board.
- A regulator could read without preparing for
  surprise.
- A sales team can use to answer enterprise
  customer governance questions.
- A future CAO succeeding you (in some
  hypothetical year-3) would inherit cleanly.

Substance, not theatre.
