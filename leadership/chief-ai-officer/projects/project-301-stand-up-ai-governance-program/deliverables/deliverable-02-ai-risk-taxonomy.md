# Deliverable 02 — AI Risk Taxonomy

**Target days:** 11–18 of the 90-day arc.
**Length:** ≤ 2 pages.
**Module integration:** mod-103 §2 (AI risk
taxonomy).

---

## What this deliverable is

Northrise-specific AI risk taxonomy. Drawn from
mod-103 Ex-01 pattern but adapted for an AI
SaaS — Northrise's risk profile is
materially different from an asset manager's
(Halverston) or insurer's (Northfield).

## What it should contain

A one-page table per mod-103 §2.2 / mod-103
Ex-01 reference:

- 7–9 top-level categories.
- For each: one-line definition, 2–4 sub-
  categories, mapping to Northrise's
  enterprise risk taxonomy (if one exists; if
  not, mapping to the operational risk
  language Northrise uses).
- "Where it applies" annotation — Northrise's
  three product lines (Agent Platform,
  Analytics, Studio) may have different
  applicability per category.

Plus a half-page reasoning note explaining:

1. The hardest call (where two risks could be
   split or combined).
2. What you deliberately left out.
3. The Northrise-specific category, if any
   (e.g., **customer-deployed risk** for the
   Agent Platform, which runs on customer
   infrastructure with customer configuration).

## Discipline applied

- **Top-level count** 7–9. Resist three-level
  taxonomies (per mod-103 §2.5).
- **Mutually exclusive** at top level.
- **Northrise-context-specific.** A taxonomy
  inherited from a financial-services
  reference won't fit; adapt.
- The **customer-deployed risk** dimension is
  worth special attention — Northrise's
  flagship product runs on customer
  infrastructure with customer-side
  configuration, which creates a control
  surface fundamentally different from
  Northrise-hosted products.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| 7–9 top-level categories | 10% |
| Mutual exclusivity at top level | 20% |
| Northrise-context adaptations | 25% |
| Sub-categories — concrete, 2–4 per category | 15% |
| Customer-deployed risk dimension addressed | 15% |
| Reasoning note — substantive | 10% |
| Length discipline — ≤ 2 pages | 5% |

## Where to find help

- mod-103 Ex-01 reference solution
  (Halverston Capital taxonomy).
- mod-103 §2 (full discipline).

The Halverston pattern is the right starting
shape; the content needs Northrise-specific
adaptation because the contexts differ
materially.
