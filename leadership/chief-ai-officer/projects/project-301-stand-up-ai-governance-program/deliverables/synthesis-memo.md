# Synthesis Memo

**Target day:** Day 90.
**Length:** ≤ 2 pages.
**Module integration:** mod-112 Ex-05 capstone
discipline.

---

## What this memo is

The synthesis that ties the seven prior
deliverables together. Not part of the formal
program artifacts — written for the CAO's own
sake, the CEO, and a future successor.

This is the document the CAO writes for the
file that says *how the program got built,
what trade-offs were made, and what would have
been done differently with hindsight*.

## What it should contain

Five sections:

### Section 1 — What the program is at day 90

Brief synthesis of the foundation laid. The
seven artifacts together comprise the program;
the synthesis names what *substantively*
exists vs. what is named-but-not-yet-
operational.

### Section 2 — Trade-offs made

Specific trade-offs the CAO made during the 90
days. Examples:

- Operating model choice (centralised vs.
  hub-and-spoke vs. federated).
- Trust architecture build/buy decision.
- Scope of the risk taxonomy.
- Year-1 hiring sequence.

For each major trade-off: alternatives
considered; the decision; why; what would
change the decision.

### Section 3 — What I would do differently

The mod-112 §6.3 honesty discipline applied at
day 90. What, in hindsight, would have been
done differently?

For a 90-day-old program, the items will be
small (no multi-year errors yet) but real:
sequencing choices; specific framings; one or
two pieces of foundation that should have been
built first.

At least three substantive items.

### Section 4 — What I'm worried about

Honest naming of what is fragile in the
foundation laid. Where is the program exposed?
What might surprise the CAO in Q2?

### Section 5 — The handoff condition

If the CAO had to hand the program over today,
what would the successor need to know? What
would they inherit cleanly; what would they
inherit messily?

This section calibrates the mod-112 §6.4
*being replaceable* discipline against the
reality of a 90-day-old program.

## Discipline applied

- Honesty per the mod-112 §6.3 peer-CAO test.
- "What I would do differently" must be
  substantive.
- "What I'm worried about" must name real
  concerns, not theoretical risks.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| What the program is — honest synthesis | 15% |
| Trade-offs — substantive on the major decisions | 25% |
| What I would do differently — at least three items | 25% |
| What I'm worried about — real concerns | 20% |
| Handoff condition addressed | 10% |
| Length discipline — ≤ 2 pages | 5% |
