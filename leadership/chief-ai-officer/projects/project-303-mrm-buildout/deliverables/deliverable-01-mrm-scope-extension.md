# Deliverable 01 — MRM Scope Extension Policy Update

**Target:** Days 1-15. **Length:** 3-5 pages.

## What this deliverable is

A formal policy update that extends Tessera's
existing MRM policy to cover AI/ML models.
Goes to the Risk Committee for approval.
Drafted by you (CAO); CRO is the policy
owner; Audit is a recipient.

## What it should contain

1. **The scope question.** A clear definition
   of which AI/ML systems fall under MRM.
   This is harder than it sounds — vendor
   APIs, fine-tuned LLMs, RPA-with-ML,
   business-rules-with-an-ML-step are all
   edge cases.
2. **The definition of "model" for AI/ML.**
   Aligned with SR 11-7's definition where
   possible; explicit deviations and reasons.
3. **In-scope vs. out-of-scope examples.**
   Specific examples from Tessera's
   inventory.
4. **Proportionality principle.** Not all
   in-scope models receive equal MRM
   attention — proportionality is determined
   per the tiering framework (Deliverable 03).
5. **The CAO × MRM accountability split.**
   Who owns what. Aligned with mod-104.
6. **Approval and amendment process.** How
   this policy is updated as the AI
   landscape changes.

## Constraints

- The scope definition must be specific
  enough that a business-line team can
  determine on their own whether a new
  system is in-scope.
- The definition must handle vendor LLMs
  (the HR resume-screening case will be a
  specific worked example).
- The proportionality principle must reach
  into how the 28 outside-MRM models will
  be triaged.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
