# Deliverable 06 — Q4 Board Package and OCC Exam Readiness

**Target:** Days 60-85. **Length:** Board
package + 3-page exam-readiness memo.

## What this deliverable is

Two artifacts:

1. **Q4 Board package** — to the Risk
   Committee at day 75. Documents what
   Tessera has built; what's outstanding;
   what risk posture the bank has on AI/ML
   today.
2. **OCC examination readiness memo** — for
   the February exam. Goes to the CRO and to
   the CEO. Names what the OCC will likely
   examine; what Tessera will present; what
   gaps remain and how Tessera will address
   them in the response cycle.

## What the Board package should contain

- Summary of the MRM extension: scope,
  tiering, validation methodology, ongoing
  monitoring framework.
- The 42-model inventory with tier
  distribution.
- The four findings from inventory
  (Treasury, wealth management, HR vendor
  LLM, anything else).
- 90-day-arc accomplishments vs. plan.
- Outstanding gaps and the plan to close.
- The exam-readiness summary.
- Risk-decision asks (if any).

## What the OCC exam memo should contain

- Anticipated lines of inquiry for AI/ML
  governance.
- Tessera's documented approach per the six
  deliverables.
- What the OCC will likely press hardest on
  (the four findings).
- Tessera's response framing on each.
- Gaps the exam may surface; remediation
  plan posture.
- Personnel and document logistics for the
  exam.

## Constraints

- Board package length and density
  appropriate for a Risk Committee audience
  (not a deep-technical audience).
- Honest about gaps. Performative completeness
  fails Risk Committee scrutiny.
- Exam-readiness memo is a working document,
  not a press release. Internally honest.
- The four findings must be addressed in
  both artifacts.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Board package summary discipline | 20% |
| Four findings addressed honestly | 25% |
| Gaps named substantively | 15% |
| Exam-readiness memo realistic | 20% |
| Anticipated OCC inquiries substantive | 10% |
| Risk-decision asks discipline | 10% |
