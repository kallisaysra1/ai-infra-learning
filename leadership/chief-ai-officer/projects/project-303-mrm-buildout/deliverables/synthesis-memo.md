# Synthesis Memo — 90-day MRM Extension Closeout

**Target:** Days 86-90. **Length:** ≤ 3 pages.

## What this memo is

The synthesis at the close of the 90-day arc.
Goes to the CEO, the CRO, the Chair of the
Risk Committee, and the Head of Internal
Audit. Internally, this is what the CAO
writes to themselves at the conclusion of
the foundational MRM-extension work.

## What it should contain

1. **What exists now that didn't 90 days
   ago.** Crisp summary of the operational
   change.
2. **What got built vs. plan.** Where the
   90-day arc landed vs. where the planning
   conversation with the CRO assumed it
   would land.
3. **What we got wrong.** Per mod-112 §6.3
   honesty discipline. At least 2 substantive
   items.
4. **What's fragile.** Specific fragilities,
   not generic concerns.
5. **The Q1 → exam plan.** Days 91-140 (to
   the OCC exam). What needs to happen.
   Specific bandwidth implications.
6. **The CAO × MRM × Audit triangle as it
   stands today.** Per mod-104 — has the
   split-authorship boundary held up under
   90 days of real work? Where did it
   strain?

## Constraints

- "What we got wrong" must be substantive.
- Q1 plan must be specific enough to
  resource.
- The CAO × MRM × Audit observation must
  be honest. Programs that paper over
  boundary friction in this triangle do not
  survive exam cycles.

## Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| 90-day summary | 15% |
| Vs. plan analysis | 15% |
| What we got wrong substantive | 25% |
| Fragility specific | 20% |
| Q1 plan resourced | 15% |
| Boundary triangle observation | 10% |
