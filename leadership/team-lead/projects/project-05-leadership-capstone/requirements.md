# Requirements — Project 05: Leadership Capstone

This document specifies the requirements your capstone artifacts must satisfy. Requirements are prioritized using MoSCoW: **M**ust have, **S**hould have, **C**ould have, **W**on't have (this round).

Audience: assume your reviewer is your skip-level engineering director, the incoming VP, and one peer leader who has watched many capstone presentations and can spot performative writing immediately. The artifacts must hold up under all three lenses.

---

## 1. Scope of the Capstone Exercise

You are producing a portfolio that integrates the work of Projects 01-04 and presents you as a leader at the next level. The scenario:

- Your current VP is moving on. The incoming VP arrives in 6 weeks and will have a 60-minute introduction conversation with each team lead.
- You have been asked by your director to draft a change-management proposal for a non-trivial change to engineering operations. The specific change you choose to use as your worked example is up to you — examples in `playbook.md` §1.
- You will be invited to present at an internal leadership development program (15-min talk). The audience: 30-50 peer team leads and senior engineers across the company.

If your real situation differs (e.g., your current VP is not leaving), treat the project as a simulation. The artifacts must be production-quality regardless.

---

## 2. Leadership Design Doc Requirements

### Must (M)

- **M-LD1** A coherent theory-of-leadership document, not a list of accomplishments. Sections required: what I optimize for, what I've learned (specifically through Projects 01-04), what kind of team I build, what I would do differently, what's next.
- **M-LD2** "What I optimize for" must be ≤ 3 things, each one a specific priority that the reader could test against your behaviors. ("I optimize for the team's ability to maintain its judgment under deadline pressure" is concrete; "I optimize for delivering value" is not.)
- **M-LD3** "What I've learned" must reference at least 2 specific moments from Projects 01-04 by name and describe what changed in your thinking.
- **M-LD4** "What kind of team I build" must describe culture in observable behaviors, not aspirational language. ("My teams write things down before they decide" is observable; "my teams have a culture of psychological safety" is not.)
- **M-LD5** "What I would do differently" must include at least 2 specific decisions from Projects 01-04 that you would now reverse, and why.
- **M-LD6** "What's next" must name the next-level skill you do not yet have and the deliberate practice you'll use to develop it.

### Should (S)

- **S-LD1** A 1-line leadership statement that distills the document — a kind of personal "north star" sentence.
- **S-LD2** A reference to a model or framework you've made your own (Lencioni, Westrum, DORA, SPACE, Kotter, 7S, etc.) and how it shapes your practice.

### Could (C)

- **C-LD1** A "leaders I steal from" section — 2-3 leaders you've actively studied and what you take from each.

### Won't (W)

- **W-LD1** Will not include a resume. (This is a leadership doc, not a CV.)
- **W-LD2** Will not include only generic frameworks. (If your doc could be written by someone who hadn't done Projects 01-04, you've missed the point.)
- **W-LD3** Will not use humblebrag growth edges ("I sometimes care too much about quality"). Reviewer will catch.

## 3. Through-line Statement Requirements

### Must (M)

- **M-TL1** A 1-page document connecting Projects 01-04 into a single leadership story.
- **M-TL2** Must be a thesis, not a summary. Identify the through-line — the recurring theme in your decisions across the four projects.
- **M-TL3** Must name something that surprised you about the through-line.

### Won't (W)

- **W-TL1** Will not be a "here's what I did in Project N, here's what I did in Project N+1" recap.

---

## 4. Change-Management Playbook Requirements

### Must (M)

- **M-CM1** A reusable playbook covering: proposing change, communicating it, sequencing rollout, handling resistance, measuring success.
- **M-CM2** Each section includes templates / scripts (not just guidance).
- **M-CM3** Playbook applied to one *specific* hypothetical change (e.g., introducing architecture review boards, shifting on-call to central SRE, consolidating teams). The application produces a concrete rollout plan.
- **M-CM4** Uses or adapts at least one established change-management framework (Kotter 8-step, ADKAR, McKinsey 7S, Switch / Heath brothers). Cited explicitly.
- **M-CM5** Includes a "stakeholder coalition" mapping for the specific change — who supports, who opposes, who is undecided, and how each is handled.
- **M-CM6** Includes a "pre-mortem on the change" — what could go wrong, what early signals to watch.
- **M-CM7** Includes a "if it's failing, here's how I cancel" section — change initiatives die badly more often than they die well.

### Should (S)

- **S-CM1** A "what counts as success at 30 / 90 / 365 days" measurement framework.
- **S-CM2** A "how I'd write the announcement" section with a sample written announcement.
- **S-CM3** A "what I would not change" annex — the things that look like they need change but don't.

### Could (C)

- **C-CM1** A retrospective format for after the change has stabilized.

### Won't (W)

- **W-CM1** Will not be a generic Kotter restatement. (Apply, don't summarize.)

## 5. Executive Briefing Requirements

### Must (M)

- **M-EB1** A written briefing document of 3-5 pages, intended to be re-readable by the incoming VP.
- **M-EB2** Structured as a 60-minute conversation arc: first 15 minutes (your team + you), middle 30 (work + priorities + tensions), last 15 (asks + open questions).
- **M-EB3** Includes speaker notes per section — what you'd actually say, not just bullet points.
- **M-EB4** Answers explicitly: what does your team do, why does it matter, what's working, what's hard, what do you need from the VP in their first 90 days.
- **M-EB5** Includes "anticipated VP questions" with pre-baked answers (at least 5 questions).
- **M-EB6** Includes a "what success looks like in 6 months between us" framing.

### Should (S)

- **S-EB1** A 1-page version that could be sent as pre-read before the meeting.
- **S-EB2** A "what I would NOT tell the VP in the first meeting" annex — things to save for a later conversation, with reasoning.
- **S-EB3** A "tone choices" note — how you adjust delivery if the VP is engaged technical vs. focused on metrics vs. focused on org politics.

### Could (C)

- **C-EB1** A version adapted for a different incoming-leader profile (e.g., what changes if the new VP is from product instead of from engineering).

### Won't (W)

- **W-EB1** Will not include any complaints about the outgoing VP.
- **W-EB2** Will not include a deep technical primer. (Wrong audience for first meeting.)
- **W-EB3** Will not include any quantitative claim you couldn't substantiate if asked in the moment.

## 6. Video Presentation Outline Requirements

### Must (M)

- **M-VP1** A 15-minute talk outline structured by minute (m0-m15), with key points, beats, and pacing notes.
- **M-VP2** Has a thesis sentence stated within the first 2 minutes.
- **M-VP3** Has 3-5 supporting points, each anchored in a specific story or example.
- **M-VP4** Has a call-to-action or takeaway in the last 60 seconds.
- **M-VP5** Includes anticipated audience questions (≥ 5) with pre-baked answers.
- **M-VP6** Includes a visual aids list — what slides, diagrams, or props (if any). Slides are *not* the message; if you removed the slides, the talk should still work.
- **M-VP7** Designed for a *non-technical* portion of the audience to follow the entire talk.

### Should (S)

- **S-VP1** A recorded version of the talk (10-15 min). Strongly encouraged.
- **S-VP2** A "what I cut" annex — content considered and removed, with reasoning. (Demonstrates editorial discipline.)
- **S-VP3** A pre-talk warm-up routine for yourself.

### Could (C)

- **C-VP1** A "5-minute version" of the same talk for a different context (skip-level meeting, all-hands).
- **C-VP2** A "what I'd do differently if I had 60 minutes instead of 15" annex.

### Won't (W)

- **W-VP1** Will not be a "here are my accomplishments" talk.
- **W-VP2** Will not be a "here are my favorite frameworks" talk. (Apply, don't enumerate.)
- **W-VP3** Will not depend on slides for comprehension. (Audio-only listener should follow.)

## 7. Portfolio Index + Reflection Requirements

### Must (M)

- **M-PR1** An index of all deliverables from Projects 01-05 with 1-sentence descriptions and links.
- **M-PR2** A reflection section covering: what changed in your leadership during the track, what you got wrong, what your next growth edge is.
- **M-PR3** "What I got wrong" must name at least 2 specific decisions from Projects 01-04 you'd reverse, with reasoning.
- **M-PR4** "Next growth edge" must name a specific leadership skill you cannot yet do well and a concrete plan for developing it.
- **M-PR5** The growth edge must pass the "uncomfortable to say in writing" test. If your growth edge is comfortable to publish, it isn't your actual edge.

### Should (S)

- **S-PR1** A "what changed in my view of [topic]" sub-section — naming a specific topic where your thinking moved during the track.
- **S-PR2** A "models I no longer use" sub-section — frameworks or approaches you've consciously moved past.

### Could (C)

- **C-PR1** A letter to your past self at the start of the track.

### Won't (W)

- **W-PR1** Will not be a recap of accomplishments.
- **W-PR2** Will not include any reflection you wouldn't be comfortable having a peer team lead read aloud.

## 8. Cross-artifact Consistency Requirements

### Must (M)

- **M-CC1** All 5 deliverables tell the same story. The leadership philosophy in D1 shows up in the executive briefing in D3 and the presentation in D4.
- **M-CC2** The Through-line statement is reflected (not just cited) in D1, D3, and D4.
- **M-CC3** No artifact contradicts another in tone, claims, or framing.
- **M-CC4** Every artifact references at least one of the previous four projects by name and uses it as evidence — not as filler.

---

## 9. Deliverable Requirements

### Must (M)

- **M-DL1** All 5 deliverables (D1-D5) plus the Through-line Statement committed as Markdown in `deliverables/`.
- **M-DL2** Each artifact has a "limits of this" section (or equivalent) — where the artifact would *not* apply or would *not* work.
- **M-DL3** Total reading time of the package ≤ 60 minutes for an executive reader.

### Should (S)

- **S-DL1** A recorded 10-15 minute video of the presentation, linked from D4 or D5.
- **S-DL2** A 1-line personal leadership statement (north star sentence), included in D1 and D5.

### Won't (W)

- **W-DL1** Will not require any single artifact > 10 pages.

---

## 10. Constraints

- **Time:** 40 hours. Do not exceed by more than 10%.
- **Voice:** This is your voice, not the company's. Don't write in HR language.
- **Honesty:** The reflection sections are graded on honesty. Performative reflection scores 1-2.
- **Audience:** Different artifacts have different audiences. Adapt the voice. Same content, different register.
- **Real or simulated:** If you don't have a real incoming VP, simulate. The simulation must produce a real-quality artifact.

---

## 11. Out of Scope

This project does **not** cover:

- Producing new operational artifacts (those were Projects 01-04).
- Coaching others on leadership (different skill, future scope).
- Public-facing thought leadership (e.g., blog posts) — though the D4 talk could be adapted for that.
- Job-search materials. The capstone is for internal positioning, not external hiring.

Stay in scope. The temptation to add a sixth artifact ("a leadership-development curriculum for my team") is exactly the over-engineering this project teaches you to resist.
