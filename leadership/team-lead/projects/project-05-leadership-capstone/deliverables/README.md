# Deliverables — Project 05: Leadership Capstone

Submit all artifacts as Markdown files in this directory. File names are prescribed below — keep them so reviewers can find them and so cross-references between artifacts remain stable.

The grader and any peer reviewer will read **only what is in this folder.** Notes, drafts, and exploration belong in `notes/` or `drafts/` at the project root, not here.

## 2. Structural Requirements (apply to every artifact)

Each Markdown file must include the following at the top:

```markdown
**Author:** [Your name]
**Last updated:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Status:** Draft | Reviewed | Final
**Reviewed by:** [Skip-level, peer team lead, or coach name]
```

Each artifact must also include, at the bottom, a section titled exactly:

```markdown
## Limits of This
```

This is a 3-5 bullet enumeration of where the artifact would *not* apply, would *not* work, or would have to change (e.g., "This Leadership Design Doc reflects who I am in 2026; in 2 years I expect the optimize-for list will change in response to taking on a larger org"). **Reviewers grade against the presence and quality of this section** (see `rubric.md`).

## 3. Formats

- All artifacts in Markdown.
- Voice and register varies per artifact (see `../playbook.md` §15).
- No slide decks (presentation is an *outline*; recording is optional video).
- Tables for the portfolio index and any coalition mapping.
- No PowerPoint, no Word docs, no PDFs of slides.

## 5. What Reviewers Read First

In order:

1. `00-summary.md` — sets expectations, includes 1-line leadership statement
2. `02-through-line.md` — anchors the integration
3. `01-leadership-design-doc.md` — the theory
4. `06-portfolio-and-reflection.md` — the honesty
5. `04-executive-briefing.md` — the senior-peer voice
6. `03-change-management-playbook.md` — the operationalization
7. `05-presentation-outline.md` — the message landing

If the Through-line or the Leadership Design Doc is weak, every other dimension caps at ~3. Invest accordingly.

---

## 6. What Not to Submit

- Slide decks (`.pptx`, `.key`)
- A resume or CV
- Recap-style portfolio summaries ("Here's what I did in P01, here's what I did in P02...")
- "Aspirational" leadership writing not anchored in specific moments from Projects 01-04
- Humblebrag growth edges ("I sometimes care too much about quality")
- Briefings that contain complaints about previous leadership
- Any artifact whose voice could have been written by someone who had not done Projects 01-04

If you find yourself wanting to add an 8th file, you are over-building. The capstone is the integration, not the addition.

## 7. A Note on the Video

The 10-15 minute video recording is the **most-recommended** optional deliverable. The reason: the act of recording forces the artifact to land. You cannot hide behind written hedges. You cannot use "etc." or "and so on." You must say what you mean, in your own voice, looking at a camera.

If you do record:
- Aim for one usable take after 2-3 practice runs.
- Don't over-edit. Some imperfection signals the recording is real, not performed.
- Link from `06-portfolio-and-reflection.md` or `appendix-video-recording-link.md`.

If you don't record:
- Write the outline as if you will record it next week. Imagine the camera. Imagine the listener.
- The act of pretending the recording exists is itself the lesson.

A reviewer should be able to read your outline and *hear* you delivering it. If they can't, the outline isn't done.
